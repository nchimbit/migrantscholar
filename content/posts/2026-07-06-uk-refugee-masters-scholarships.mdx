## Fully Funded Master's Scholarships for Refugee Students in the UK
Fully funded master's scholarships are available for refugee students in the UK, providing an opportunity for individuals with refugee status to pursue higher education without financial burden. 
## Who Qualifies
To be eligible for these scholarships, applicants must hold one of the following visa categories:
* Refugee status
* Humanitarian Protection
* Limited Leave to Remain under the Refugee Family Reunion or Private Life Rules
* Discretionary Leave to Remain
* Leave outside the Rules

## Available Scholarships
The following fully funded master's scholarships are available for refugee students in the UK:
### The University of Oxford Weidenfeld-Hoffmann Scholarships
The University of Oxford Weidenfeld-Hoffmann Scholarships provide full tuition fees and a living costs stipend of £18,168 per annum, with eligibility open to applicants who have been awarded refugee status in the UK, and the deadline for application is November 2026. The official website URL is https://www.ox.ac.uk/admissions/graduate/fees-and-funding/graduate-scholarships/university-of-oxford-scholarships/weidenfeld-hoffmann-scholarships-and-leadership-programme.

## Application Steps
To apply for these scholarships, follow these eight steps:
1. Check the eligibility criteria for each scholarship to ensure that you qualify.
2. Review the application deadlines for each scholarship and plan accordingly.
3. Gather all required documents, including academic transcripts and proof of refugee status.
4. Complete the online application form for your chosen scholarship.
5. Submit your application and supporting documents before the deadline.
6. Wait for the scholarship committee to review your application and make a decision.
7. If successful, receive confirmation of your scholarship award and instructions on how to claim your funding.
8. Arrive at your host university and commence your master's program.

## Frequently Asked Questions
### Q: What is the definition of a refugee for the purpose of these scholarships?
A refugee is defined as an individual who has been granted refugee status or humanitarian protection in the UK. This status is typically granted to individuals who have fled their home country due to persecution, war, or natural disasters. Refugee status is usually granted after an individual has applied for asylum in the UK. 
### Q: Can I apply for multiple scholarships at the same time?
Yes, you can apply for multiple scholarships at the same time. However, it is essential to check the eligibility criteria for each scholarship to ensure that you qualify, and to review the application deadlines to avoid missing any opportunities.
### Q: What is the difference between refugee status and humanitarian protection?
Refugee status is granted to individuals who have a well-founded fear of persecution in their home country, while humanitarian protection is granted to individuals who have been affected by conflict, war, or natural disasters.

Yes, you can apply for a scholarship if you have limited leave to remain in the UK, but you must meet the eligibility criteria for the scholarship and have a valid visa that allows you to study in the UK.
