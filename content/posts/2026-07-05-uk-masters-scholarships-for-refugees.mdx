## Fully Funded Masters Scholarships for Refugee Students in the UK
Fully funded Masters scholarships are available for refugee students in the UK, offering a unique opportunity for those who have been displaced to pursue higher education and rebuild their lives.

## Who Qualifies
To be eligible for these scholarships, refugee students must hold a specific visa category, including:
* Refugee visa
* Humanitarian Protection visa
* Discretionary Leave to Remain visa
* Leave to Remain under Article 3 of the European Convention on Human Rights
* Section 67 of the Immigration Act 2016 visa
* Section 1 of the Refugee Convention 1951 visa

## Available Scholarships

## Application Steps
1. Check the eligibility criteria for each scholarship to ensure you meet the requirements.
2. Prepare your application materials, including your CV, personal statement, and academic transcripts.
3. Submit your application through the official website of the scholarship provider.
4. Ensure you have a valid passport and visa to study in the UK.
5. Meet the English language requirement, which may include taking an IELTS or TOEFL test.
6. Provide proof of financial support, which may include a scholarship award letter or evidence of personal savings.
7. Apply for a student visa, which may include providing biometric data and attending an interview at a UK visa application centre.
8. Register with the university and attend any required orientation or induction events.

## Frequently Asked Questions
What is the deadline for applying for the UK Government Chevening Scholarship?
The deadline for applying for the UK Government Chevening Scholarship is November 2026, and applicants are advised to submit their applications well in advance to avoid any technical issues. The scholarship is highly competitive, and only a limited number of awards are available each year.

Can I apply for more than one scholarship at the same time?

What is the difference between a refugee visa and a humanitarian protection visa?
A refugee visa and a humanitarian protection visa are both types of visas issued to individuals who are fleeing persecution or violence in their home country. However, a refugee visa is typically issued to individuals who meet the definition of a refugee under the Refugee Convention 1951, while a humanitarian protection visa is issued to individuals who do not meet the definition of a refugee but are still in need of protection.

How do I apply for a student visa to study in the UK?
To apply for a student visa to study in the UK, you must submit an online application and provide biometric data, such as fingerprints. You may also be required to attend an interview at a UK visa application centre. The application process can take several weeks, so it is essential to apply well in advance of your course start date.

Can I work part-time while studying in the UK on a student visa?
Yes, you can work part-time while studying in the UK on a student visa, but you must ensure you do not exceed the maximum number of hours permitted. The maximum number of hours you can work varies depending on the type of course you are studying and the level of study.

What is the cost of living in the UK for international students?

How do I register with the university and attend orientation events?
