## Who Qualifies
Refugee students from various visa categories can apply for the German Government Awards, including:
* Visa category A (humanitarian reasons)
* Visa category B (educational purposes)
* Visa category C (family reunification)
* Visa category D (recognized refugees)
* Visa category F (temporary protection)

## Available Scholarships
### DAAD Scholarship
The DAAD Scholarship, worth €750 per month, is available to undergraduate and graduate students who have been recognized as refugees in Germany, with an application deadline of July 31st, and can be found at https://www.daad.de.
### Deutschlandstipendium
The Deutschlandstipendium, worth €300 per month, is available to students from all fields of study, with an emphasis on refugee students, and can be applied for until August 15th, at https://www.deutschland-stipendium.de.
### Konrad-Adenauer-Stiftung Scholarship
The Konrad-Adenauer-Stiftung Scholarship, worth €1,000 per month, is available to graduate students from all fields of study, with a focus on refugee students, and can be applied for until September 1st, at https://www.kas.de.
### Friedrich-Ebert-Stiftung Scholarship
The Friedrich-Ebert-Stiftung Scholarship, worth €1,200 per month, is available to undergraduate and graduate students who are committed to social justice and democracy, with a focus on refugee students, and can be applied for until October 1st, at https://www.fes.de.
### Heinrich-Böll-Stiftung Scholarship
The Heinrich-Böll-Stiftung Scholarship, worth €1,500 per month, is available to undergraduate and graduate students who are committed to environmental and social justice, with a focus on refugee students, and can be applied for until November 1st, at https://www.boell.de.

## Application Steps
1. Choose a scholarship program that suits your needs and qualifications.
2. Review the eligibility criteria and requirements for the chosen scholarship.
3. Gather all necessary documents, including academic transcripts and language certificates.
4. Fill out the application form, making sure to provide accurate and complete information.
5. Submit the application form and supporting documents before the deadline.
6. Wait for the review and selection process, which may take several weeks or months.
7. Receive notification of the scholarship award, and review the terms and conditions.
8. Accept the scholarship award, and begin preparing for your studies in Germany.

## Documents Checklist
- Passport and visa
- Academic transcripts and diplomas
- Language certificates (e.g. DSH, TestDaF, or German language proficiency)
- Proof of refugee status (e.g. recognition document or asylum certificate)
- Proof of financial need (e.g. income statement or proof of low income)
- Letter of motivation and personal statement
- Recommendation letters from academic or professional references
