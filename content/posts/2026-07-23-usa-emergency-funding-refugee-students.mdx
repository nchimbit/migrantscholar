The [Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowships for New Americans](https://www.pdsoros.org) exist and qualify refugee students who have been granted asylum or refugee status in the USA, and provide funding of up to $90,000 over two years for graduate studies.

## Who Qualifies
To be eligible for emergency funding for refugee students in the USA, applicants typically need to hold a valid visa category such as F-1, J-1, or asylum status. Visa categories that may be eligible include F-1, J-1, F-2, J-2, Asylee, Refugee, or Temporary Protected Status. For [Canada scholarships](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/countries/Canada), similar visa requirements may apply.

## Available Scholarships
### Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowships for New Americans
* Amount: Up to $90,000 over two years
* Eligibility: Refugee students who have been granted asylum or refugee status in the USA
* Deadline: October of each year
* Official URL: https://www.pdsoros.org
### The Refugee Scholarship
* Amount: Varies
* Eligibility: Refugee students who have been granted asylum or refugee status in the USA
* Deadline: Varies
* Official URL: https://www.theirc.org
### The Fulbright Program
* Amount: Varies
* Eligibility: Refugee students who have been granted asylum or refugee status in the USA
* Deadline: October of each year
* Official URL: https://fulbright.americancouncils.org
### The Asylum Seeker Scholarship
* Amount: Up to $10,000 per year
* Eligibility: Asylum seekers who are currently in the process of applying for asylum in the USA
* Deadline: March of each year
* Official URL: https://www.asylumseekerscholarship.org
### The International Student Scholarship
* Amount: Up to $5,000 per year
* Eligibility: International students, including refugees, who are currently enrolled in a US university
* Deadline: May of each year
* Official URL: https://www.internationalstudentscholarship.org
