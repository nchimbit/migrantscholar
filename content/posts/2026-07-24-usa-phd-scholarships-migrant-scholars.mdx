The Fulbright Foreign Student Program exists and qualifies migrant scholars from Middle Eastern countries for fully funded PhD programs in the USA, with funding amounting to full tuition, a living stipend, and health insurance. 
Migrant scholars from countries such as Iran, Iraq, and Turkey can apply for this prestigious award.

## Who Qualifies
Migrant scholars from Middle Eastern countries who hold a valid visa, such as an F-1 or J-1 visa, are eligible to apply for fully funded PhD programs in the USA.

## Application Steps
1. Research and select a PhD program at a US university that aligns with your research interests and goals.
2. Review the eligibility criteria and application requirements for the program.
3. Prepare and submit a strong application package, including transcripts, letters of recommendation, and a personal statement.
4. Take the required standardized tests, such as the GRE or TOEFL.
5. Submit a research proposal that outlines your research goals and objectives.
6. Apply for a visa, such as an F-1 or J-1 visa, if you are not already residing in the USA.
7. Submit a financial aid application, if required by the university.
8. Participate in an interview, if required by the university.

## Documents Checklist
* Transcripts and diplomas from all previous academic institutions
* Letters of recommendation from academic or professional mentors
* Personal statement or research proposal
* Standardized test scores, such as GRE or TOEFL
* Visa application documents, such as a passport and proof of financial support
* Financial aid application, if required by the university
