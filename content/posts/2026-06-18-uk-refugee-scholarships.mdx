## Fully Funded Scholarships for Refugee Students in the UK
If you are a refugee student seeking to pursue higher education in the UK, there are several fully funded scholarships available to support your academic endeavors. These scholarships can cover tuition fees, living expenses, and other costs associated with studying in the UK. In this article, we will provide an overview of the eligibility criteria, application process, and available scholarships for refugee students in the UK.

## Who Qualifies
To be eligible for fully funded scholarships for refugee students in the UK, you must hold a specific visa category, including:
* Refugee status
* Humanitarian protection
* Discretionary leave
* Leave outside the rules
* Section 67 of the Immigration Act 2016
* Section 8 of the Immigration Act 1971
* Article 3 of the European Convention on Human Rights
* Other forms of protection granted by the UK government

## Available Scholarships
### The Allan and Nesta Ferguson Charitable Trust Scholarship
The Allan and Nesta Ferguson Charitable Trust Scholarship provides £10,000 per year for three years to refugee students pursuing undergraduate or postgraduate studies in the UK. To be eligible, you must be a refugee or have been granted humanitarian protection in the UK. The deadline for application is usually in April, and you can find more information on the [official website](https://www.soas.ac.uk/scholarships/allan-and-nesta-ferguson-scholarship/).
### The UK Government Chevening Scholarship
The UK Government Chevening Scholarship offers a full scholarship covering tuition fees, living expenses, and other costs to refugee students pursuing postgraduate studies in the UK. To be eligible, you must have been granted refugee status in the UK and meet the academic requirements of your chosen university. The deadline for application is usually in November, and you can find more information on the [official website](https://www.chevening.org/).
### The UNHCR Scholarship
The UNHCR Scholarship provides $10,000 to $20,000 per year to refugee students pursuing undergraduate or postgraduate studies in the UK. To be eligible, you must be a refugee recognized by UNHCR and have been admitted to a UK university. The deadline for application is usually in March, and you can find more information on the [official website](https://www.unhcr.org/scholarships.html).
### The University of London Scholarship
The University of London Scholarship offers a full scholarship covering tuition fees, living expenses, and other costs to refugee students pursuing undergraduate or postgraduate studies at the University of London. To be eligible, you must have been granted refugee status in the UK and meet the academic requirements of the University of London. The deadline for application is usually in May, and you can find more information on the [official website](https://london.ac.uk/scholarships).
### The British Council Scholarship
The British Council Scholarship provides £10,000 to £20,000 per year to refugee students pursuing postgraduate studies in the UK. To be eligible, you must have been granted refugee status in the UK and meet the academic requirements of your chosen university. The deadline for application is usually in February, and you can find more information on the [official website](https://www.britishcouncil.org/scholarships).

## Application Steps
1. Check your eligibility for the scholarship and ensure you meet the requirements.
2. Choose the scholarship you wish to apply for and review the application guidelines.
3. Prepare all required documents, including your passport, visa, and academic transcripts.
4. Submit your application online or by post, depending on the scholarship requirements.
5. Wait for the application to be processed and reviewed by the scholarship committee.
6. If shortlisted, attend an interview or complete an assessment task.
7. Receive notification of the scholarship outcome and review the terms and conditions.
8. Accept the scholarship offer and begin your studies in the UK.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. What is the deadline for applying for fully funded scholarships for refugee students in the UK?
The deadline varies depending on the scholarship, but most applications close between February and May.
2. Can I apply for multiple scholarships at the same time?
Yes, you can apply for multiple scholarships, but ensure you meet the eligibility criteria and follow the application guidelines for each scholarship.
3. What documents do I need to submit with my scholarship application?
You will need to submit a range of documents, including your passport, visa, academic transcripts, and proof of English language proficiency.
4. How do I prove my refugee status for scholarship applications?
You can provide a copy of your refugee status letter or a document from the Home Office confirming your status.
5. Can I apply for a scholarship if I have been granted discretionary leave?
Yes, some scholarships are available to students with discretionary leave, but check the eligibility criteria for each scholarship.
6. How long does it take to process a scholarship application?
The processing time varies depending on the scholarship, but most applications take 2-6 months to process.
7. Can I appeal a rejected scholarship application?
Yes, you can appeal a rejected application, but you must follow the appeals process outlined by the scholarship committee.
8. What happens if I am awarded a scholarship but my visa application is rejected?
You may need to defer your scholarship or reapply for a visa, but check the terms and conditions of your scholarship for more information.
