## Introduction to Australian Government Fully Funded Awards
The Australian Government offers fully funded awards to migrant and refugee students to support their education in Australia, providing them with an opportunity to pursue their academic goals without financial burdens. These awards are available for both undergraduate and postgraduate studies, covering various fields of study. By applying for these awards, migrant and refugee students can achieve their academic aspirations and build a better future for themselves.

## Who Qualifies
To be eligible for the Australian Government fully funded awards, migrant and refugee students must hold one of the following visa categories:
* Permanent Humanitarian Visa (subclass 200, 201, 202, 203, 204, 206, 207, 208, 209, 210, 211, 212, 213, 214, 215, 216, 217, 818, or 820)
* Temporary Protection Visa (subclass 785)
* Safe Haven Enterprise Visa (subclass 790)
* Temporary Humanitarian Concern Visa (subclass 786)
* Protection Visa (subclass 866)

## Available Scholarships
The Australian Government offers various fully funded awards to support the education of migrant and refugee students. Here are five real scholarships available:
### Australia Awards Scholarships
The Australia Awards Scholarships provide full tuition fees, living expenses, and other allowances up to AUD 30,000 per year. To be eligible, applicants must hold a valid visa, meet the English language proficiency requirements, and apply for a participating university. The deadline for applications is typically in April each year. For more information, visit [https://www.australiaawards.gov.au](https://www.australiaawards.gov.au).
### Research Training Program (RTP) Scholarships
The Research Training Program (RTP) Scholarships offer full tuition fees and living expenses up to AUD 28,597 per year. Eligible applicants must apply for a higher degree by research at a participating university, meet the English language proficiency requirements, and hold a valid visa. The deadline for applications varies depending on the university. For more information, visit [https://www.education.gov.au/research-training-program](https://www.education.gov.au/research-training-program).
### Australian Government Research Training Program (AGRTP) International Scholarships
The Australian Government Research Training Program (AGRTP) International Scholarships provide full tuition fees and living expenses up to AUD 28,597 per year. To be eligible, applicants must apply for a higher degree by research at a participating university, meet the English language proficiency requirements, and hold a valid visa. The deadline for applications is typically in August each year. For more information, visit [https://www.unisa.edu.au/Research/Research-degrees/Scholarships/](https://www.unisa.edu.au/Research/Research-degrees/Scholarships/).
### Endeavour Scholarships and Fellowships
The Endeavour Scholarships and Fellowships offer full tuition fees, living expenses, and travel allowances up to AUD 140,000. Eligible applicants must apply for a postgraduate program or a research fellowship at a participating university, meet the English language proficiency requirements, and hold a valid visa. The deadline for applications is typically in June each year. For more information, visit [https://www.australiaawards.gov.au/endavour](https://www.australiaawards.gov.au/endavour).
### International Postgraduate Research Scholarship (IPRS)
The International Postgraduate Research Scholarship (IPRS) provides full tuition fees and living expenses up to AUD 28,597 per year. To be eligible, applicants must apply for a higher degree by research at a participating university, meet the English language proficiency requirements, and hold a valid visa. The deadline for applications varies depending on the university. For more information, visit [https://www.education.gov.au/international-postgraduate-research-scholarship](https://www.education.gov.au/international-postgraduate-research-scholarship).

## Application Steps
To apply for the Australian Government fully funded awards, migrant and refugee students can follow these steps:
1. Check the eligibility criteria for each scholarship and ensure they meet the requirements.
2. Choose a participating university and program of study.
3. Prepare the necessary documents, including academic transcripts, language proficiency test results, and visa documents.
4. Submit an application for the scholarship through the university's website or the official government website.
5. Apply for a valid visa to study in Australia.
6. Meet the English language proficiency requirements.
7. Provide proof of financial support, if required.
8. Submit the application before the deadline.

## Documents Checklist
Migrant and refugee students should prepare the following documents to apply for the Australian Government fully funded awards:
* Academic transcripts
* Language proficiency test results (e.g. IELTS, TOEFL)
* Visa documents (e.g. passport, visa approval letter)
* Proof of financial support (e.g. bank statement, scholarship award letter)
* Personal statement or research proposal
* Letters of recommendation
* Medical certificate
* Police certificate
