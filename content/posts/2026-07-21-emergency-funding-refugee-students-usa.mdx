The IIE Scholarship exists for refugee students, providing up to $25,000 in emergency funding to pursue higher education in the USA.

## Who Qualifies
Refugee students with the following visa categories are eligible to apply: F-1, J-1, or have a pending asylum application. Additionally, students with a Temporary Protected Status (TPS) or Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA) status may also be eligible.

## Available Scholarships
### IIE Scholarship
* Amount: up to $25,000
* Eligibility: Refugee students
* Deadline: varies
* URL: https://www.iie.org/
### The Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowships for New Americans
* Amount: up to $90,000
* Eligibility: New Americans, including refugees and asylum seekers
* Deadline: November 1
* URL: https://pdsoros.org/
### The Scholarships for Refugee and Asylum Seeker Students at University of California, Berkeley
* Amount: full tuition
* Eligibility: Refugee and asylum seeker students
* Deadline: December 15
* URL: https://www.berkeley.edu/
### The Weiser Center for Emerging Democracies Scholarship
* Amount: up to $10,000
* Eligibility: Students from emerging democracies, including refugees
* Deadline: February 15
* URL: https://www.ii.umich.edu/wced
### The Fulbright Scholarship
* Amount: full tuition and living expenses
* Eligibility: International students, including refugees
* Deadline: varies
* URL: https://fulbright.americancouncils.org/

## Application Steps
1. Check eligibility for the scholarship
2. Review the application requirements
3. Gather all necessary documents
4. Complete the online application form
5. Submit the application on time
6. Wait for the application to be reviewed
7. Receive notification of the application decision
8. Accept the scholarship and review the terms and conditions

## Documents Checklist
* Passport
* Visa
* I-20 form
* Proof of financial need
* Academic transcripts
* Letters of recommendation
* Personal statement
* Language proficiency test results (e.g. TOEFL or IELTS)
