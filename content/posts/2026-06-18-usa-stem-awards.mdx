## Introduction to Migrant Student Awards in the USA for STEM Fields
As a migrant student in the USA, pursuing a degree in a STEM field can be a challenging yet rewarding experience. If you are a migrant student looking for financial assistance to support your education in the USA, there are several awards and scholarships available to help you achieve your goals. These awards are specifically designed to recognize and support the academic achievements of migrant students in STEM fields, providing them with the necessary resources to excel in their studies.

## Who Qualifies for Migrant Student Awards
To qualify for migrant student awards in the USA, you must hold a valid visa that allows you to study in the country. The following visa categories are typically eligible for these awards:
* F-1 visa: for academic students
* J-1 visa: for exchange visitors
* H-4 visa: for dependents of H-1B visa holders
* L-2 visa: for dependents of L-1 visa holders
* O-3 visa: for dependents of O-1 visa holders
* TD visa: for dependents of TN visa holders
It is essential to check the specific eligibility criteria for each award, as some may have additional requirements or restrictions.

## Available Scholarships for Migrant Students in STEM Fields
There are numerous scholarships available to support migrant students in STEM fields. Here are five real scholarships that you can consider:
### The Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowships for New Americans
The Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowships for New Americans provides up to $90,000 in funding to support graduate studies in the USA. To be eligible, you must be a new American (a green card holder or naturalized citizen) who is 30 years old or younger. The deadline for application is November 1st, and you can find more information on their official website: https://www.pdsoros.org.
### The National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellowship Program
The National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellowship Program provides up to $138,000 in funding to support graduate studies in STEM fields. To be eligible, you must be a US citizen, national, or permanent resident, and be pursuing a graduate degree in a STEM field. The deadline for application is October 22nd, and you can find more information on their official website: https://www.nsf.gov/funding/pgm_summ.jsp?pims_id=6201.
### The Google Lime Scholarship
The Google Lime Scholarship provides up to $10,000 in funding to support undergraduate or graduate studies in computer science or a related field. To be eligible, you must have a disability and be pursuing a degree in computer science or a related field. The deadline for application is December 6th, and you can find more information on their official website: https://limeconnect.com/opportunities/google-lime-scholarship-program.
### The Microsoft Scholarship Program
The Microsoft Scholarship Program provides up to $20,000 in funding to support undergraduate or graduate studies in computer science or a related field. To be eligible, you must be pursuing a degree in computer science or a related field, and have a minimum GPA of 3.0. The deadline for application is March 31st, and you can find more information on their official website: https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/diversity/programs/microsoft-scholarship.
### The Intel Scholarship Program
The Intel Scholarship Program provides up to $10,000 in funding to support undergraduate or graduate studies in STEM fields. To be eligible, you must be pursuing a degree in a STEM field, and have a minimum GPA of 3.0. The deadline for application is May 15th, and you can find more information on their official website: https://www.intel.com/content/www/us/en/education/intel-scholarship-program.html.
