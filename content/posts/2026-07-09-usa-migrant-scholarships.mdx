## Fully Funded Awards for Migrant Students in the USA
Migrant students in the USA with F-1, J-1, or other visa categories can apply for fully funded awards to support their higher education, covering full tuition, living costs, and other expenses.

## Who Qualifies
Migrant students with the following visa categories qualify for these awards:
* F-1 visa for academic students
* J-1 visa for exchange visitors
* F-2 visa for dependents of F-1 visa holders
* J-2 visa for dependents of J-1 visa holders
* Other visa categories, including refugees and asylum seekers

## Available Scholarships
The following are some of the top fully funded scholarships available for migrant students in the USA:
### The Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowships for New Americans
The Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowships for New Americans provide $100,000 over two years, covering tuition and living costs for graduate students. To be eligible, applicants must be migrant students who are 30 years old or younger and are pursuing a graduate degree in the USA. The deadline for application is November 2026. For more information, visit https://www.pdsoros.org/.
### The Gates Scholarship
The Gates Scholarship provides full tuition coverage, along with living costs and other expenses, for undergraduate students. To be eligible, applicants must be migrant students who are high school seniors and are pursuing a bachelor's degree in the USA. The deadline for application is September 2026. For more information, visit https://www.thegatesscholarship.org/.
### The Fulbright Foreign Student Program
The Fulbright Foreign Student Program provides full tuition coverage, along with living costs and other expenses, for graduate students. To be eligible, applicants must be migrant students who are pursuing a master's or doctoral degree in the USA. The deadline for application varies by country. For more information, visit https://fulbrightforeignstudentprogram.org/.
### The Soros Justice Fellowship
The Soros Justice Fellowship provides $100,000 to $150,000 over 18 months, covering living costs and other expenses for students pursuing social justice projects. To be eligible, applicants must be migrant students who are 30 years old or younger and are pursuing a project that advances social justice in the USA. The deadline for application is July 2027. For more information, visit https://www.opensocietyfoundations.org/.
### The National Immigration Law Center Fellowship
The National Immigration Law Center Fellowship provides $50,000 to $75,000 over one year, covering living costs and other expenses for students pursuing immigration law projects. To be eligible, applicants must be migrant students who are pursuing a project that advances the rights of migrant students in the USA. The deadline for application is May 2027. For more information, visit https://www.nilc.org/.

## Application Steps
To apply for these awards, follow these steps:
1. Review the eligibility criteria to ensure you qualify for the award.
2. Prepare your application materials, including transcripts, letters of recommendation, and a personal statement.
3. Submit your application online through the official website.
4. Ensure you have all required documents, including a valid passport and visa.
5. Apply for multiple awards to increase your chances of receiving funding.
6. Follow up with the award committee to confirm receipt of your application.
7. Prepare for and take required exams, such as the TOEFL or GRE.
8. Receive and review the award letter, and respond to accept the award.

## Documents Checklist
To apply for these awards, you will need the following documents:
* Valid passport and visa
* Transcripts from previous academic institutions
* Letters of recommendation from academic or professional mentors
* Personal statement outlining your academic and career goals
* Proof of English language proficiency, such as TOEFL or IELTS scores
* Standardized test scores, such as SAT or GRE
