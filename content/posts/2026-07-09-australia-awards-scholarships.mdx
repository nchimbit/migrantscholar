## Australian Government Awards for Migrant Students
The Australian Government Awards are available to migrant students who qualify for various visa categories, providing them with the opportunity to pursue higher education in Australia.

## Who Qualifies
To be eligible for the Australian Government Awards, migrant students must hold one of the following visa categories:
* Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485)
* Skilled Independent visa (subclass 189)
* Skilled Nominated visa (subclass 190)
* Skilled Regional (Provisional) visa (subclass 489)
* Student visa (subclass 500)
* Permanent visa holders who have been residing in Australia for less than 5 years
* New Zealand citizens who have been residing in Australia for less than 5 years

## Available Scholarships
The Australian Government offers various scholarships to support migrant students in their pursuit of higher education. Here are five available scholarships:
### Australian Awards Scholarship
The Australian Awards Scholarship covers full tuition fees, living costs, and return airfare, with a total value of up to $30,000 per year. To be eligible, students must hold a Student visa (subclass 500) and be enrolled in a full-time undergraduate or postgraduate degree. The deadline for application is 30 April 2027. For more information, visit [https://www.australiaawards.gov.au/](https://www.australiaawards.gov.au/).
### Endeavour Postgraduate Scholarship
The Endeavour Postgraduate Scholarship provides $140,000 per year for up to 2 years, covering tuition fees, living costs, and travel expenses. To be eligible, students must hold a Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) and be enrolled in a postgraduate degree. The deadline for application is 15 November 2026. For more information, visit [https://www.australiaawards.gov.au/endavour](https://www.australiaawards.gov.au/endavour).
### Research Training Program (RTP) Scholarship
The Research Training Program (RTP) Scholarship covers full tuition fees and provides a stipend of $28,597 per year for up to 3 years. To be eligible, students must hold a Student visa (subclass 500) and be enrolled in a research-based postgraduate degree. The deadline for application is 31 October 2026. For more information, visit [https://www.education.gov.au/research-training-program](https://www.education.gov.au/research-training-program).
### Australia Awards Pacific Scholarships
The Australia Awards Pacific Scholarships cover full tuition fees, living costs, and return airfare, with a total value of up to $30,000 per year. To be eligible, students must hold a Student visa (subclass 500) and be enrolled in a full-time undergraduate or postgraduate degree. The deadline for application is 28 February 2027. For more information, visit [https://www.australiaawards.gov.au/pacific](https://www.australiaawards.gov.au/pacific).
### Destination Australia Scholarship
The Destination Australia Scholarship provides $15,000 per year for up to 4 years, covering living costs and tuition fees. To be eligible, students must hold a Student visa (subclass 500) and be enrolled in a full-time undergraduate or postgraduate degree at a regional university. The deadline for application is 30 June 2027. For more information, visit [https://www.studyregional.au/destination-australia](https://www.studyregional.au/destination-australia).

## Application Steps
To apply for the Australian Government Awards, migrant students must follow these steps:
1. Check eligibility criteria and ensure they hold a valid visa category.
2. Research and choose the desired scholarship and course.
3. Prepare all required documents, including academic transcripts and English language proficiency test results.
4. Submit an online application through the official website.
5. Wait for the application to be processed and reviewed.
6. Receive and review the offer letter, if successful.
7. Accept the offer and enroll in the course.
8. Maintain a satisfactory academic record and comply with visa conditions.

## Documents Checklist
To apply for the Australian Government Awards, migrant students must provide the following documents:
* Valid passport
* Visa grant notice
* Academic transcripts
* English language proficiency test results (e.g. IELTS or TOEFL)
* Proof of English language proficiency (if applicable)
* Proof of financial support (if applicable)
* Health insurance certificate
* Police certificate (if applicable)

## Frequently Asked Questions
Here are some frequently asked questions from migrant students:
1. What is the minimum English language proficiency requirement for the Australian Government Awards?
The minimum English language proficiency requirement for the Australian Government Awards is IELTS 6.5 or TOEFL 79. However, some scholarships may have higher requirements, so it's essential to check the specific eligibility criteria.
2. Can I apply for the Australian Government Awards if I have a dependent?
Yes, migrant students with dependents can apply for the Australian Government Awards. However, they must provide additional documentation, such as proof of financial support and health insurance for their dependents.
3. How long does it take to process the application?
The processing time for the Australian Government Awards application can take several weeks to several months. It's essential to submit the application well in advance of the deadline to ensure timely processing.
4. Can I apply for multiple scholarships at the same time?
Yes, migrant students can apply for multiple scholarships at the same time. However, they must ensure they meet the eligibility criteria for each scholarship and provide all required documents.
5. What happens if my application is unsuccessful?
If the application is unsuccessful, migrant students can reapply in the next round. However, they must ensure they meet the eligibility criteria and provide all required documents.
6. Can I change my course or institution after receiving the scholarship?
Yes, migrant students can change their course or institution after receiving the scholarship. However, they must obtain approval from the scholarship provider and ensure they meet the eligibility criteria for the new course or institution.
7. How do I maintain my visa conditions while studying in Australia?
Migrant students must comply with their visa conditions, including maintaining a satisfactory academic record, attending classes regularly, and not working more than 20 hours per week.
8. What support services are available for migrant students in Australia?
Migrant students can access various support services, including academic support, language support, and cultural support. They can contact their institution's international student office or the Australian Government's support services for assistance.
