The Weidenfeld-Hoffmann Scholarship and Leadership Programme exists and is open to asylum seekers, providing full funding of £14,533 to £17,733 for master's courses at the University of Oxford. 
The programme supports students from developing and emerging economies, including those with refugee status, to pursue their academic and professional goals in the UK. 
To be eligible for this and other STEM scholarships, asylum seekers must meet specific visa categories and requirements, which are outlined below.

## Who Qualifies
Asylum seekers with certain visa categories, including those with refugee status, humanitarian protection, or discretionary leave, may be eligible for STEM scholarships in the UK. 
These categories include:
- Refugee status
- Humanitarian protection
- Discretionary leave
- Limited leave to remain
- Other forms of leave that are not for study purposes

## Application Steps
1. Check the eligibility criteria for each scholarship
2. Review the required documents and prepare them in advance
3. Submit an online application for the chosen scholarship
4. Provide academic transcripts and certificates
5. Write a personal statement and CV
6. Secure references from academic or professional contacts

8. Follow up with the scholarship provider to confirm the application status

## Documents Checklist
- Academic transcripts and certificates
- Personal statement and CV
- References from academic or professional contacts
- Proof of English language proficiency
- Proof of financial support
- Copy of passport and visa (if applicable)
