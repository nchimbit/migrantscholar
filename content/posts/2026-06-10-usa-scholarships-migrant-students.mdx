## Introduction to Scholarships for Migrant Students in the USA
The United States of America offers a wide range of scholarships to migrant students pursuing higher education in 2026. These scholarships are designed to support students from diverse backgrounds and help them achieve their academic and career goals. In this blog post, we will explore the various scholarships available to migrant students in the USA, including eligibility criteria, application deadlines, and the application process.

## Who Qualifies for Scholarships
To be eligible for scholarships in the USA, migrant students must hold a valid visa. The following visa categories qualify for scholarships:
* F-1 visa (Student Visa)
* J-1 visa (Exchange Visitor Visa)
* H-4 visa (Dependent Visa)
* L-2 visa (Dependent Visa)
* O-3 visa (Dependent Visa)
* K-1 visa (Fiancé(e) Visa)
* Asylum or Refugee status

## Available Scholarships
### The Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowships for New Americans
The Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowships for New Americans offers $90,000 in funding to support graduate education. To be eligible, applicants must be 30 years old or younger and hold a valid visa. The application deadline is November 1, 2026. For more information, visit [https://www.pdsoros.org/](https://www.pdsoros.org/).
### The Hispanic Scholarship Fund
The Hispanic Scholarship Fund offers $500 to $5,000 in funding to support undergraduate and graduate education. To be eligible, applicants must be of Hispanic heritage and hold a minimum GPA of 3.0. The application deadline is February 15, 2026. For more information, visit [https://www.hsf.net/](https://www.hsf.net/).
### The Asian American and Native American Pacific Islander-Serving Institutions (AANAPISI) Scholarship
The Asian American and Native American Pacific Islander-Serving Institutions (AANAPISI) Scholarship offers $2,500 to $5,000 in funding to support undergraduate and graduate education. To be eligible, applicants must be of Asian American or Pacific Islander descent and hold a minimum GPA of 2.5. The application deadline is September 1, 2026. For more information, visit [https://www.apiasf.org/](https://www.apiasf.org/).
### The Fulbright Foreign Student Program
The Fulbright Foreign Student Program offers funding to support graduate education. To be eligible, applicants must hold a valid visa and have a minimum GPA of 3.0. The application deadline is October 1, 2026. For more information, visit [https://foreign.fulbrightonline.org/](https://foreign.fulbrightonline.org/).
### The PEO International Peace Scholarship
The PEO International Peace Scholarship offers $5,000 to $10,000 in funding to support graduate education. To be eligible, applicants must be an international student and hold a minimum GPA of 3.0. The application deadline is December 15, 2026. For more information, visit [https://www.peointernational.org/](https://www.peointernational.org/).

## Application Steps
1. Research and select a scholarship that aligns with your academic and career goals.
2. Review the eligibility criteria and ensure you meet the requirements.
3. Gather all required documents, including transcripts, letters of recommendation, and visa documents.
4. Create an account on the scholarship website and start the application process.
5. Complete the application form and upload all required documents.
6. Submit the application and pay the application fee, if required.
7. Wait for the application to be reviewed and processed.
8. Receive notification of the application outcome and follow up on any additional requirements.
