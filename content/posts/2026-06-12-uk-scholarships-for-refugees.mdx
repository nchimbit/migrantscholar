## Introduction to Fully Funded UK Scholarships for Refugee Students
If you are a refugee student seeking to pursue higher education in the UK, there are several fully funded scholarships available to support your academic journey. These scholarships are designed to provide financial assistance to refugee students, helping them to overcome the barriers that may prevent them from accessing quality education. By applying for these scholarships, refugee students can focus on their studies without worrying about the financial burden of tuition fees and living expenses.

## Who Qualifies for UK Scholarships for Refugee Students
To be eligible for fully funded UK scholarships, refugee students must hold a valid visa that falls under one of the following categories:
* Refugee visa
* Humanitarian protection visa
* Discretionary leave visa
* Leave outside the rules visa
* Section 67 leave visa
* Section 4(1) leave visa
* Asylum seeker with a pending application for refugee status

## Available Scholarships for Refugee Students in the UK
### The Sanctuary Scholarship
The Sanctuary Scholarship, offered by the University of West London, provides full tuition fee coverage, which is approximately £13,000 per year, and a £1,000 stipend for living expenses. To be eligible, applicants must be a refugee or asylum seeker with a strong academic record and a commitment to community service. The deadline for application is 31st July 2026, and more information can be found at https://www.uwl.ac.uk/scholarships/sanctuary-scholarship.

## Application Steps for UK Scholarships for Refugee Students
1. Check the eligibility criteria for each scholarship and ensure you meet the requirements.
2. Gather all required documents, including academic certificates, English language proficiency test results, and proof of refugee status.
3. Choose the scholarships you wish to apply for and review the application guidelines.
4. Apply online through the official website of the university or organization offering the scholarship.
5. Submit a personal statement outlining your academic goals, community service experience, and reasons for applying for the scholarship.
6. Provide two academic references from previous teachers or lecturers.
7. Upload all required documents, including a copy of your passport and visa.
8. Submit your application before the deadline and wait for the outcome.
