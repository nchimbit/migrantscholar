The Commonwealth Scholarship Commission offers the Commonwealth Master's Scholarship to international students from eligible Commonwealth countries, providing full tuition fees and a monthly stipend of £1,084 for the duration of the master's programme at the University of Edinburgh.

## Who Qualifies
International students seeking to study at the University of Edinburgh must apply for a UK Tier 4 visa, which is categorized into two types: Tier 4 (General) student visa and Tier 4 (Child) student visa. Students from outside the UK and EU who are 16 or older can apply for a Tier 4 (General) student visa, while students under 16 can apply for a Tier 4 (Child) student visa.

### Commonwealth Master's Scholarship
* Amount: Full tuition fees and £1,084 monthly stipend
* Eligibility: International students from eligible Commonwealth countries
* Deadline: Varies by country
* Official URL: https://cscuk.fcdo.gov.uk/scholarships/commonwealth-masters-scholarships/

### Chevening Scholarship
* Amount: Full tuition fees and £1,084 monthly stipend
* Eligibility: International students from Chevening-eligible countries
* Deadline: November 1st
* Official URL: https://www.chevening.org/

### Gates Cambridge Scholarship
* Amount: Full tuition fees and £17,500 stipend per annum
* Eligibility: International students from any country outside the UK
* Deadline: December 2nd for US citizens and October 13th for international students
* Official URL: https://www.gatescambridge.org/

### Edinburgh Global Scholarship
* Amount: £5,000 to £10,000
* Eligibility: International students from any country outside the EU
* Deadline: Varies by programme
* Official URL: https://www.ed.ac.uk/student-funding/postgraduate/international/edinburgh-global

### Saltire Scholarship
* Amount: £8,000
* Eligibility: International students from Canada, China, India, Japan, Pakistan, or the USA
* Deadline: May 31st
* Official URL: https://www.scotland.org/study/saltire-scholarships

## Application Steps
1. Choose a course at the University of Edinburgh and meet the admission requirements.
2. Apply for admission to the University of Edinburgh through their website.
3. Receive an unconditional offer from the University of Edinburgh.
4. Apply for a UK Tier 4 visa through the UK Government website.
5. Gather the required documents, including a valid passport and proof of English proficiency.
6. Pay the visa application fee and the Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS).
7. Attend a biometric appointment and provide fingerprints.
8. Wait for the visa decision and receive the biometric residence permit (BRP) card.

## Documents Checklist
* Valid passport
* Proof of English proficiency (e.g. IELTS or TOEFL certificate)
* Unconditional offer letter from the University of Edinburgh
* Proof of funds (e.g. bank statement)
* Academic transcripts and certificates
* Proof of language proficiency in English (if required)

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. What is the cost of a UK Tier 4 visa application? The cost is £348 for a standard application and £454 for a priority application.
2. Can I work part-time with a Tier 4 visa? Yes, you can work up to 20 hours per week during term-time and full-time during holidays.
3. Do I need to take an English language proficiency test? Yes, if English is not your first language, you need to provide proof of English proficiency.
4. Can I apply for a Tier 4 visa from outside the UK? Yes, you can apply from outside the UK, but you must meet the eligibility criteria.
5. How long does it take to process a Tier 4 visa application? The standard processing time is 3-8 weeks, but priority applications are processed within 5-7 working days.
6. Can I bring my family with me to the UK? Yes, but they must apply for a separate visa as your dependents.
7. What is the Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS)? The IHS is a mandatory fee that provides access to the UK's National Health Service (NHS).
8. Can I apply for a Tier 4 visa extension? Yes, if you need to extend your stay in the UK, you can apply for a visa extension.
