## Fully Funded German Academic Exchange Scholarships for Refugee Students in Germany
The German Academic Exchange Scholarships are available for refugee students in STEM fields, providing full tuition and living costs for the duration of their studies in Germany. 

## Who Qualifies
Refugee students with the following visa categories qualify for the German Academic Exchange Scholarships:
* Subsidiary protection
* Asylum
* Refugee status
* Temporary protection
* Humanitarian admission

## Available Scholarships
### DAAD Scholarship for Refugee Students
The DAAD Scholarship for Refugee Students covers €750 per month for living costs, plus tuition fees, for students pursuing a bachelor's or master's degree in STEM fields. Eligible students must have a refugee status and be enrolled in a German university. The deadline for application is November 2026. For more information, visit https://www.daad.de/.

### Deutschlandstipendium for Refugee Students
The Deutschlandstipendium for Refugee Students provides €300 per month for living costs, plus tuition fees, for students pursuing a bachelor's or master's degree in STEM fields. Eligible students must have a subsidiary protection or asylum status and be enrolled in a German university. The deadline for application is June 2027. For more information, visit https://www.deutschlandstipendium.de/.

### Friedrich Ebert Foundation Scholarship
The Friedrich Ebert Foundation Scholarship covers €750 per month for living costs, plus tuition fees, for students pursuing a bachelor's or master's degree in STEM fields. Eligible students must have a refugee status and be enrolled in a German university. The deadline for application is March 2027. For more information, visit https://www.fes.de/.

### Heinrich Böll Foundation Scholarship
The Heinrich Böll Foundation Scholarship provides €750 per month for living costs, plus tuition fees, for students pursuing a bachelor's or master's degree in STEM fields. Eligible students must have a subsidiary protection or asylum status and be enrolled in a German university. The deadline for application is September 2026. For more information, visit https://www.boell.de/.

### Konrad Adenauer Foundation Scholarship
The Konrad Adenauer Foundation Scholarship covers €750 per month for living costs, plus tuition fees, for students pursuing a bachelor's or master's degree in STEM fields. Eligible students must have a refugee status and be enrolled in a German university. The deadline for application is October 2026. For more information, visit https://www.kas.de/.

## Application Steps
1. Check the eligibility criteria for each scholarship and ensure you qualify.
2. Gather all required documents, including your visa, passport, and academic transcripts.
3. Fill out the application form for each scholarship, either online or by mail.
4. Submit your application before the deadline, along with all required documents.
5. Wait for the selection committee to review your application and make a decision.
6. If selected, receive confirmation and instructions on how to receive your scholarship.
7. Use the scholarship to fund your studies in Germany.
8. Report back to the scholarship provider on your academic progress and any changes to your status.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. What is the deadline for applying to the DAAD Scholarship for Refugee Students?
The deadline for applying to the DAAD Scholarship for Refugee Students is November 2026. It is essential to submit your application before this date to be considered for the scholarship. You can find more information on the official DAAD website.
2. Can I apply for multiple scholarships at the same time?
Yes, you can apply for multiple scholarships at the same time. However, you must ensure that you meet the eligibility criteria for each scholarship and submit separate applications for each one.
3. What documents do I need to apply for the Deutschlandstipendium for Refugee Students?
To apply for the Deutschlandstipendium for Refugee Students, you will need to submit your valid visa or residence permit, passport, academic transcripts, and proof of admission to a German university.
4. How do I know if I am eligible for the Friedrich Ebert Foundation Scholarship?
To know if you are eligible for the Friedrich Ebert Foundation Scholarship, you must check the eligibility criteria on the official Friedrich Ebert Foundation website. You can also contact the foundation directly to ask about your eligibility.
5. Can I use the Konrad Adenauer Foundation Scholarship to fund my studies in a field other than STEM?
No, the Konrad Adenauer Foundation Scholarship is only available for students pursuing a degree in STEM fields. If you are studying a different field, you are not eligible for this scholarship.
6. How long does it take to receive a decision on my scholarship application?
The time it takes to receive a decision on your scholarship application varies depending on the scholarship provider. It can take several weeks or even months to receive a decision, so it is essential to apply early.
7. Can I renew my scholarship for another year?
Yes, some scholarships can be renewed for another year, but this depends on the scholarship provider and your academic performance. You must check the scholarship conditions to see if renewal is possible.
8. What happens if I do not receive my scholarship payment on time?
If you do not receive your scholarship payment on time, you should contact the scholarship provider directly to report the issue. They will investigate and resolve the problem as soon as possible.
