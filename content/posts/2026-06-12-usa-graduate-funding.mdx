## Introduction to USA Graduate Funding for Asylum Seekers
As an asylum seeker in the United States, you may be wondering if you are eligible for graduate funding to pursue your higher education goals. The good news is that there are several scholarship opportunities available to support your graduate studies. These scholarships can help cover the costs of tuition, living expenses, and other fees associated with pursuing a graduate degree in the USA.

## Who Qualifies for Graduate Funding
To be eligible for graduate funding as an asylum seeker in the USA, you must hold a specific visa category. The following visa categories qualify:
* Asylee (AS)
* Refugee (RE)
* Special Immigrant Visa (SIV)
* Temporary Protected Status (TPS)
* Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA)
* Deferred Enforced Departure (DED)
* Withholding of Removal (W/OR)

## Available Scholarships for Asylum Seekers
As an asylum seeker, you can apply for the following graduate scholarships:
### The Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowship for New Americans
The Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowship for New Americans provides up to $90,000 in funding to support graduate studies. To be eligible, applicants must be a New American (born in the USA to immigrant parents, a naturalized citizen, or a green card holder). The deadline for application is November 1st. For more information, visit [https://www.pdsoros.org/](https://www.pdsoros.org/).
### The Fulbright Foreign Student Program
The Fulbright Foreign Student Program offers funding to support graduate studies in the USA, with coverage amounts varying depending on the program. Eligibility criteria include being a non-US citizen and having a strong academic record. The deadline for application varies by country. For more information, visit [https://foreign.fulbrightonline.org/](https://foreign.fulbrightonline.org/).
### The Asylum Seeker Scholarship
The Asylum Seeker Scholarship provides up to $10,000 in funding to support graduate studies. To be eligible, applicants must be asylum seekers or refugees who have been admitted to a graduate program in the USA. The deadline for application is June 30th. For more information, visit [https://www.asylumseekerscholarship.org/](https://www.asylumseekerscholarship.org/).
### The Open Society Foundations Scholarship
The Open Society Foundations Scholarship offers up to $20,000 in funding to support graduate studies. Eligibility criteria include being a non-US citizen and having a strong academic record. The deadline for application is March 15th. For more information, visit [https://www.opensocietyfoundations.org/](https://www.opensocietyfoundations.org/).
### The Weidenfeld-Hoffmann Scholarship
The Weidenfeld-Hoffmann Scholarship provides up to $30,000 in funding to support graduate studies. To be eligible, applicants must be non-US citizens who have been admitted to a graduate program in the USA. The deadline for application is December 15th. For more information, visit [https://www.graduate.ox.ac.uk/weidenfeld](https://www.graduate.ox.ac.uk/weidenfeld).

## Application Steps for Graduate Funding
To apply for graduate funding as an asylum seeker in the USA, follow these steps:
1. Research and select a graduate program that aligns with your academic and career goals.
2. Review the eligibility criteria for the scholarship you are applying for.
3. Prepare and submit your application, including all required documents.
4. Take any required standardized tests, such as the GRE or TOEFL.
5. Obtain letters of recommendation from academic or professional references.
6. Submit your application by the deadline.
7. Follow up with the scholarship committee to confirm receipt of your application.
8. Receive and review your application decision, and follow any additional instructions.

## Documents Checklist
To apply for graduate funding as an asylum seeker in the USA, you will need to submit the following documents:
* Passport
* Visa or immigration documents (AS, RE, SIV, TPS, DACA, DED, W/OR)
* Academic transcripts
* Letters of recommendation
* Standardized test scores (GRE, TOEFL)
* Personal statement or essay
* Resume or CV
