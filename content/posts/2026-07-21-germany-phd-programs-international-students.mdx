# Germany PhD
The German Academic Exchange Service (DAAD) offers a fully funded PhD scholarship worth €1,200 per month to international students, including migrants, refugees, and asylum seekers, who wish to pursue their PhD studies at top universities in Germany.

## Who Qualifies
To qualify for fully funded PhD programs in Germany, applicants must hold a master's degree in a relevant field and meet the required language proficiency. Visa categories include student visas, job seeker visas, and family reunification visas. International students, including [migrants](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/by-eligibility/migrants), [refugees](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/by-eligibility/refugees), and [asylum seekers](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/by-eligibility/asylum-seekers), are eligible to apply.

## Scholarships
### DAAD Scholarship
* Amount: €1,200 per month
* Eligibility: International students with a master's degree
* Deadline: varies
* [Official URL](https://www.daad.de/en/)
### Konrad-Adenauer-Stiftung Scholarship
* Amount: €1,200 per month
* Eligibility: International students with a master's degree
* Deadline: July 15th
* [Official URL](https://www.kas.de/en/)
### Heinrich Böll Foundation Scholarship
* Amount: €1,200 per month
* Eligibility: International students with a master's degree
* Deadline: September 1st
* [Official URL](https://www.boell.de/en)
### Friedrich Ebert Foundation Scholarship
* Amount: €1,200 per month
* Eligibility: International students with a master's degree
* Deadline: November 30th
* [Official URL](https://www.fes.de/en/)
### Deutscher Akademischer Austauschdienst (DAAD) Helmut-Schmidt-Programme
* Amount: €1,200 per month
* Eligibility: International students with a master's degree
* Deadline: July 31st
* [Official URL](https://www.daad.de/en/)

## Application Steps
1. Choose a PhD program at a top university in Germany
2. Check the eligibility criteria and required documents
3. Submit an online application
4. Take language proficiency tests, such as IELTS or TOEFL
5. Get a letter of acceptance from a German university
6. Apply for a student visa
7. Fill out the scholarship application form
8. Submit all required documents before the deadline

## Documents Checklist
* Master's degree certificate
* Transcript of records
* Language proficiency certificate
* Letter of motivation
* Letter of recommendation
* Proof of financial resources
* Health insurance certificate

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. What is the language of instruction in German universities?
 German universities offer many programs in English, but knowledge of German is also required.
2. Can I work part-time while studying in Germany?
 Yes, international students can work part-time for up to 20 hours per week.
3. Do I need to take a language proficiency test?
 Yes, most German universities require language proficiency tests, such as IELTS or TOEFL.
4. How can I apply for a student visa?
 You can apply for a student visa at the German embassy or consulate in your home country.
5. What is the cost of living in Germany?
 The cost of living in Germany varies, but on average, it costs around €800-€1,000 per month.
6. Can I bring my family to Germany?
 Yes, international students can bring their families to Germany, but they need to apply for a family reunification visa.
7. How long does it take to process a student visa?
 It usually takes 2-3 months to process a student visa.
8. What kind of health insurance do I need?
 International students need to have health insurance that covers them in Germany.
