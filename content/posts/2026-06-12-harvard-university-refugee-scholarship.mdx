## Introduction to Refugee Scholarship Program at Harvard University
The USA offers various scholarship programs for refugees and asylum seekers, with one of the most prestigious being the Refugee Scholarship Program at Harvard University, which provides opportunities for undergraduate studies. This program is specifically designed for individuals who have been displaced due to conflict, persecution, or natural disasters and are seeking to pursue higher education in the United States. By providing financial assistance and academic support, the program enables refugees to achieve their academic goals and build a better future.

## Who Qualifies
To be eligible for the Refugee Scholarship Program at Harvard University, applicants must hold one of the following visa categories:
* Refugee (Type I-94 with a stamp indicating refugee status)
* Asylum status (Type I-94 with a stamp indicating asylum status)
* Special Immigrant Visa (SIV) for Iraqis or Afghans who worked for the US government
* Special Immigrant Visa (SIV) for Syrians who worked for the US government
* Temporary Protected Status (TPS)
* DACA (Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals)

## Available Scholarships
The following scholarships are available for refugees and asylum seekers pursuing undergraduate studies at Harvard University:
### The Harvard University Refugee Scholarship
The Harvard University Refugee Scholarship provides full tuition coverage, which is around $53,496 per year, to refugees and asylum seekers pursuing undergraduate studies. To be eligible, applicants must demonstrate financial need, academic excellence, and a strong commitment to their community. The deadline for application is December 15th, and more information can be found on the official website: https://college.harvard.edu/.
### The Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowships for New Americans
The Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowships for New Americans provide $90,000 over two years to support undergraduate studies. To be eligible, applicants must be refugees or asylum seekers who have been naturalized as US citizens or have a green card. The deadline for application is November 1st, and more information can be found on the official website: https://www.pdsoros.org/.
### The Refugee Education Assistance Program (REAP)
The Refugee Education Assistance Program (REAP) provides up to $5,000 per year to support undergraduate studies. To be eligible, applicants must be refugees or asylum seekers who have been in the US for less than five years. The deadline for application is October 15th, and more information can be found on the official website: https://www.acf.hhs.gov/orr/.
### The International Student Scholarship
The International Student Scholarship provides $10,000 per year to support undergraduate studies. To be eligible, applicants must be international students, including refugees and asylum seekers, who demonstrate financial need and academic excellence. The deadline for application is January 1st, and more information can be found on the official website: https://www.harvard.edu/.
### The Harvard University Committee on General Scholarships
The Harvard University Committee on General Scholarships provides various scholarships to support undergraduate studies, including the Refugee Scholarship. To be eligible, applicants must demonstrate financial need and academic excellence. The deadline for application is February 1st, and more information can be found on the official website: https://college.harvard.edu/.

## Application Steps
To apply for the Refugee Scholarship Program at Harvard University, follow these steps:
1. Check your eligibility and visa status to ensure you meet the requirements.
2. Review the available scholarships and their eligibility criteria.
3. Prepare your application documents, including transcripts, letters of recommendation, and a personal statement.
4. Submit your application through the online portal.
5. Complete the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) to demonstrate financial need.
6. Take the SAT or ACT exam to demonstrate academic excellence.
7. Submit your test scores and transcripts to the admissions office.
8. Attend an interview with the admissions committee, if required.
