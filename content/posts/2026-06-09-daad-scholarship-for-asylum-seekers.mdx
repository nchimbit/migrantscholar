Yes, the DAAD Scholarship is available to asylum seekers who wish to pursue higher education in Germany. The German Academic Exchange Service (DAAD) offers a range of scholarships to international students, including those with refugee status. Who qualifies for the DAAD Scholarship? To be eligible, you must be an international student or asylum seeker with a strong academic record, proficiency in German or English, and a clear motivation for studying in Germany. Here are some DAAD Scholarships available to international students and asylum seekers: 1. Development-Related Postgraduate Courses (EPOS) - covers tuition fees, living expenses, and travel costs - deadline: varies depending on the course, 2. Master's Programmes in Germany for International Students - covers tuition fees and living expenses - deadline: October 1st, 2026, 4. PhD Programmes in Germany for International Students - covers tuition fees, living expenses, and travel costs - deadline: varies depending on the programme, 5. Language Course Grants - covers language course fees and living expenses - deadline: April 30th, 2026, and 6. Short-Term Research Grants - covers research expenses and living costs - deadline: varies depending on the grant. To apply for the DAAD Scholarship, follow these steps: Step 1: Choose your programme and university, Step 2: Check the eligibility criteria and deadlines, Step 3: Gather required documents, Step 4: Submit your application online, Step 5: Wait for the selection result. Here is a checklist of documents you may need to apply: passport, birth certificate, academic transcripts, language proficiency certificate, motivation letter, and referee reports.
