As a refugee or migrant student seeking to pursue postgraduate studies in Germany, you are likely wondering if there are any fully funded scholarship programs available to support your academic and research endeavors. The answer is yes, the DAAD Fully Funded Scholarship Program is an excellent opportunity for you to explore, offering a wide range of scholarships for graduate studies in Germany, covering various fields of study and research opportunities. With the DAAD scholarship, you can focus on your studies and research without worrying about the financial burden, and take the first step towards a successful academic and professional career in Germany.

## Introduction to DAAD Scholarship
The DAAD Fully Funded Scholarship Program is a prestigious scholarship program offered by the German Academic Exchange Service (DAAD), aimed at supporting international students, including refugees and migrant students, to pursue their postgraduate studies in Germany. The program provides a unique opportunity for students to gain academic and research experience in a world-class environment, and to become part of a vibrant academic community.

## Who Qualifies
To be eligible for the DAAD Fully Funded Scholarship Program, you must meet certain requirements, including:
* Holding a refugee or migrant status
* Having a bachelor's or master's degree in a relevant field of study
* Meeting the language requirements (German or English)
* Having a strong academic record and research potential
* Being admitted to a German university or research institution
The following visa categories are eligible to apply:
* Subsidiary protection
* Refugee status
* Asylum seeker
* Humanitarian visa
* Family reunification visa
* Student visa

## Scholarship Opportunities
Here are five real scholarships offered by the DAAD, each with its own coverage amount, eligibility criteria, deadline, and official website URL:
### 1. Development-Related Postgraduate Courses (EPOS)
The EPOS scholarship covers a monthly stipend of 750 euros, plus travel and accommodation expenses, and is eligible for students from developing countries who have a bachelor's degree in a relevant field of study. The deadline for application is August 31st, and the official website URL is https://www.daad.de/en/study-and-research-in-germany/scholarships/development-related-postgraduate-courses-epos/.
### 2. Helmut-Schmidt-Programme (HSP)
The HSP scholarship covers a monthly stipend of 750 euros, plus travel and accommodation expenses, and is eligible for students from developing countries who have a bachelor's degree in a relevant field of study. The deadline for application is July 31st, and the official website URL is https://www.daad.de/en/study-and-research-in-germany/scholarships/helmut-schmidt-programme-hsp/.
### 3. Graduate School Scholarship Programme (GSSP)
The GSSP scholarship covers a monthly stipend of 1,200 euros, plus travel and accommodation expenses, and is eligible for students who have a master's degree in a relevant field of study. The deadline for application is October 15th, and the official website URL is https://www.daad.de/en/study-and-research-in-germany/scholarships/graduate-school-scholarship-programme-gssp/.
### 4. Research Grants (Bi-nationally Supervised Doctoral Degrees)
The Research Grants scholarship covers a monthly stipend of 1,200 euros, plus travel and accommodation expenses, and is eligible for students who have a master's degree in a relevant field of study. The deadline for application is April 15th, and the official website URL is https://www.daad.de/en/study-and-research-in-germany/scholarships/research-grants-bi-nationally-supervised-doctoral-degrees/.
### 5. Short-Term Research Grants (Doctoral Studies)
The Short-Term Research Grants scholarship covers a monthly stipend of 1,200 euros, plus travel and accommodation expenses, and is eligible for students who have a master's degree in a relevant field of study. The deadline for application is March 15th, and the official website URL is https://www.daad.de/en/study-and-research-in-germany/scholarships/short-term-research-grants-doctoral-studies/.
