## Introduction to Fully Funded University Scholarships in Canada
Canada is known for its welcoming environment for international students, including refugees and asylum seekers. The country provides various scholarship programs to support these students in achieving their academic goals. Fully funded university scholarships in Canada cover tuition fees, living expenses, and other study-related costs, making it easier for refugee and asylum seeker students to focus on their studies.

## Who Qualifies
To be eligible for fully funded university scholarships in Canada as a refugee or asylum seeker, you must fall under one of the following visa categories:
* Refugee claimant
* Convention refugee
* Protected person
* Humanitarian-protected person
* Asylum seeker with a study permit
* Refugee with a study permit
These categories are subject to change, and it's essential to check the official government website for the most up-to-date information.

## Application Process
To apply for fully funded university scholarships in Canada, follow these steps:
1. Research available scholarships and their eligibility criteria.
2. Check the website of your chosen university for scholarship information.
3. Prepare your application documents, including transcripts, language proficiency test results, and letters of recommendation.
4. Submit your application through the university's online portal or by mail.
5. Wait for the scholarship committee to review your application.
6. Receive an email notification of the scholarship decision.
7. Accept the scholarship offer and begin the study permit application process.
8. Register for classes and start your academic journey in Canada.

## Documents Checklist
When applying for fully funded university scholarships in Canada, make sure to have the following documents:
* Passport
* Study permit
* Language proficiency test results (e.g., IELTS or TOEFL)
* Transcripts
* Letters of recommendation
* Letter of admission from the university
* Proof of financial support (if required)
