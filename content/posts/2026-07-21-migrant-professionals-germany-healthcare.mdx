# Germany Jobs
The KfW Student Loan scholarship exists for migrant professionals in the healthcare sector in Germany, with funding of up to €650 per month. This guide will help navigate the job market in Germany for migrant professionals in the healthcare sector. To find a job in Germany, it is essential to have the right visa category, such as a job seeker visa, work visa, or Blue Card EU. 

## Who Qualifies
Migrant professionals with a visa under categories such as a job seeker visa, work visa, Blue Card EU, or residence permit for the purpose of employment are eligible for various scholarships and job opportunities in the healthcare sector in Germany. They can apply for [fully funded scholarships](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/by-funding/fully-funded) to pursue their education and career in Germany.

## Available Scholarships
### KfW Student Loan
* Amount: up to €650 per month
* Eligibility: Migrant professionals in the healthcare sector
* Deadline: Varies
* Official URL: https://www.kfw.de/
### DAAD Scholarship for Healthcare Professionals
* Amount: €750 per month
* Eligibility: Migrant professionals in the healthcare sector with a bachelor's degree
* Deadline: October 31st
* Official URL: https://www.daad.de/
### Erasmus Mundus Scholarship for Master in Healthcare
* Amount: €1,000 per month
* Eligibility: Migrant professionals in the healthcare sector with a bachelor's degree
* Deadline: December 15th
* Official URL: https://ec.europa.eu/programmes/erasmus-mundus/
### Heinrich Böll Foundation Scholarship
* Amount: €750 per month
* Eligibility: Migrant professionals in the healthcare sector with a bachelor's degree
* Deadline: March 1st
* Official URL: https://www.boell.de/
### Friedrich Ebert Foundation Scholarship
* Amount: €650 per month
* Eligibility: Migrant professionals in the healthcare sector with a bachelor's degree
* Deadline: November 30th
* Official URL: https://www.fes.de/

## Application Steps
1. Research the job market in Germany and find job openings in the healthcare sector.
2. Check the eligibility criteria for the scholarship and job opening.
3. Prepare the necessary documents, such as a resume and cover letter.
4. Apply for the job and scholarship through the official website or email.
5. Wait for the response from the employer and scholarship provider.
6. Prepare for the interview and assessment.
7. Receive the job offer and scholarship award letter.
8. Apply for the visa and residence permit.
