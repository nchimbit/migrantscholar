Typically, applicants must have a minimum GPA of 2.0 and be enrolled or accepted in an accredited US college or university. Some scholarships may have additional requirements, such as financial need or a specific field of study. <br><br>Available Scholarships for Migrant Students: <br>1. The Hispanic Scholarship Fund: Offers $500 to $5,000 to Hispanic students, including migrant students, pursuing higher education. Deadline: February 15th. <br>5. Deadline: March 31st. <br><br>Step-by-Step Application Process: <br>1. Research and review the scholarship eligibility criteria. <br>2. Gather required documents, such as transcripts, letters of recommendation, and proof of immigration status. <br>3. Fill out and submit the scholarship application form before the deadline. <br>4. Follow up with the scholarship provider to confirm receipt of your application. <br><br>Documents Checklist: <br>- Transcripts <br>- Letters of recommendation <br>- Proof of immigration status <br>- Proof of financial need (if required) <br>- Personal statement or essay <br>- Proof of enrollment or acceptance in an accredited US college or university <br><br>Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs): <br>1.
