The Chevening Scholarship exists and qualifies Indian migrants in the healthcare sector with up to £18,000 funding. 

## Who Qualifies
To work in the UK healthcare sector, Indian migrants must qualify under the Tier 2 visa categories, including General, Intra-Company Transfer, Minister of Religion, or Sportsperson. 

### Chevening Scholarship
* Amount: Up to £18,000
* Eligibility: Indian migrants with a bachelor's degree and at least two years of work experience
* Deadline: November 1, 2026
* Official URL: [www.chevening.org](http://www.chevening.org)

### Commonwealth Scholarship
* Amount: Up to £19,000
* Eligibility: Indian migrants with a master's or PhD degree in the healthcare sector
* Deadline: December 1, 2026
* Official URL: [cscuk.fcdo.gov.uk](http://cscuk.fcdo.gov.uk)

### Felix Scholarship
* Amount: Up to £15,000
* Eligibility: Indian migrants with a master's or PhD degree in the healthcare sector
* Deadline: January 20, 2027
* Official URL: [www.felixscholarship.org](http://www.felixscholarship.org)

### Wellcome Trust Scholarship
* Amount: Up to £30,000
* Eligibility: Indian migrants with a PhD degree in the healthcare sector
* Deadline: March 15, 2027
* Official URL: [wellcome.org](http://wellcome.org)

### UK Scholarship for Healthcare Professionals
* Amount: Up to £10,000
* Eligibility: Indian migrants with a bachelor's degree in the healthcare sector
* Deadline: April 1, 2027
* Official URL: [www.healthcare-scholarship.org.uk](http://www.healthcare-scholarship.org.uk)

## Application Steps
1. Check eligibility for the desired scholarship and visa category
2. Prepare required documents, including a valid passport and academic certificates
3. Fill out the online application form for the scholarship and visa

6. Wait for the application to be processed and a decision to be made

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. What is the difference between a Tier 2 General visa and a Tier 2 Intra-Company Transfer visa?
The main difference is that the Tier 2 General visa is for those with a job offer in the UK, while the Tier 2 Intra-Company Transfer visa is for those being transferred to a UK branch of their current employer.
2. Can I bring my family with me to the UK on a Tier 2 visa?
Yes, you can bring your family with you to the UK, but they must meet the relevant eligibility criteria and apply for a family visa.
3. How long does it take to process a Tier 2 visa application?
The processing time can vary, but it typically takes around 3-8 weeks.
4. Can I switch to a different visa category once I am in the UK?
Yes, you can switch to a different visa category, but you must meet the relevant eligibility criteria and apply for the new visa before your current one expires.
5. What is the salary requirement for a Tier 2 visa?
The salary requirement varies, but it is typically at least £25,600 per year.
6. Can I work part-time on a Tier 2 visa?
Yes, you can work part-time on a Tier 2 visa, but you must meet the relevant eligibility criteria and ensure that your main job is still in the UK.
7. How do I apply for a National Insurance number?
You can apply for a National Insurance number by contacting the National Insurance number helpline or by visiting a Jobcentre Plus office.
8. What are the requirements for health insurance on a Tier 2 visa?
You must have health insurance that covers you for the duration of your stay in the UK.
