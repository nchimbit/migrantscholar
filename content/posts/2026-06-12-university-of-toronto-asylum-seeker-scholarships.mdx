## Introduction to University of Toronto Scholarships for Asylum Seekers
The University of Toronto offers various scholarships to asylum seekers and refugees who wish to pursue undergraduate and graduate programs in Canada. Asylum seekers and refugees can apply for these scholarships to help cover the costs of tuition, living expenses, and other fees associated with studying in Canada. The University of Toronto is committed to providing opportunities for students from all backgrounds, including those who have been displaced due to conflict or persecution.

## Who Qualifies
To be eligible for University of Toronto scholarships, asylum seekers and refugees must meet specific visa category requirements. The following visa categories are eligible:
* Convention Refugees
* Protected Persons
* Refugee Claimants
* Asylum Seekers with a positive Pre-Removal Risk Assessment (PRRA)
* Asylum Seekers with a positive Refugee Claim

## Available Scholarships
The University of Toronto offers a range of scholarships to support asylum seekers and refugees in their academic pursuits. Here are five available scholarships:
### University of Toronto Scholarship for Asylum Seekers
The University of Toronto Scholarship for Asylum Seekers provides $10,000 per year for four years to students pursuing undergraduate studies. To be eligible, students must be asylum seekers or refugees with a strong academic record and demonstrated financial need. The deadline to apply is February 1, 2027. For more information, visit [https://www.utsc.utoronto.ca/~mgmt/scholarships](https://www.utsc.utoronto.ca/~mgmt/scholarships).
### Mastercard Foundation Scholars Program
The Mastercard Foundation Scholars Program provides full tuition coverage, living expenses, and other fees for graduate students. To be eligible, students must be asylum seekers or refugees with a strong academic record and demonstrated leadership potential. The deadline to apply is January 10, 2027. For more information, visit [https://www.mastercardscholars.utoronto.ca/](https://www.mastercardscholars.utoronto.ca/).
### Ontario Graduate Scholarship
The Ontario Graduate Scholarship provides $15,000 per year for two years to graduate students. To be eligible, students must be asylum seekers or refugees with a strong academic record and demonstrated research potential. The deadline to apply is March 1, 2027. For more information, visit [https://www.sgs.utoronto.ca/funding/ontario-graduate-scholarship/](https://www.sgs.utoronto.ca/funding/ontario-graduate-scholarship/).
### University of Toronto Fellowship
The University of Toronto Fellowship provides $20,000 per year for four years to graduate students. To be eligible, students must be asylum seekers or refugees with a strong academic record and demonstrated research potential. The deadline to apply is February 15, 2027. For more information, visit [https://www.sgs.utoronto.ca/funding/university-of-toronto-fellowship/](https://www.sgs.utoronto.ca/funding/university-of-toronto-fellowship/).
### Lester B. Pearson International Scholarship
The Lester B. Pearson International Scholarship provides full tuition coverage, living expenses, and other fees for undergraduate students. To be eligible, students must be asylum seekers or refugees with a strong academic record and demonstrated leadership potential. The deadline to apply is January 15, 2027. For more information, visit [https://www.pearson.utoronto.ca/](https://www.pearson.utoronto.ca/).

## Application Steps
To apply for University of Toronto scholarships, follow these steps:
1. Review the eligibility criteria and requirements for each scholarship.
2. Gather all required documents, including transcripts, letters of reference, and proof of immigration status.
3. Submit an online application to the University of Toronto.
4. Upload all required documents to the online application portal.
5. Pay the application fee (if required).
6. Submit a personal statement outlining academic goals and reasons for pursuing studies at the University of Toronto.
7. Submit a language proficiency test score (if required).
8. Track the application status online.

## Documents Checklist
To apply for University of Toronto scholarships, you will need to provide the following documents:
* Transcripts from all previous academic institutions
* Letters of reference from academic or professional referees
* Proof of immigration status (e.g. refugee claimant document, permanent resident card)
* Language proficiency test score (if required)
* Personal statement outlining academic goals and reasons for pursuing studies at the University of Toronto
* Resume or CV
* Proof of financial need (if required)
