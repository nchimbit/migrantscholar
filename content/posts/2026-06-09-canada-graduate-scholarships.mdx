## Introduction to Canada Graduate Scholarships for International Students
Canada offers various graduate scholarships to international students, including asylum seekers, to pursue their academic dreams in the country. The Canada Graduate Scholarships program provides financial support to students who are pursuing a master's or doctoral degree at a Canadian university. If you are an international student, including an asylum seeker, looking to pursue graduate studies in Canada, you may be eligible for these scholarships.

## Who Qualifies
To qualify for Canada graduate scholarships, international students, including asylum seekers, must hold a valid Canadian study permit or be eligible for a study permit under one of the following visa categories:
* Study permit holder
* Permanent resident
* Protected person
* Refugee claimant
* Person in need of protection
* Convention refugee
* Humanitarian-protected person abroad
* Asylum seeker with a valid work permit

## Available Scholarships
Canada offers various scholarships to international students, including asylum seekers, to pursue graduate studies. Here are five real scholarships:
### Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarships
The Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarships provide $50,000 per year for three years to students pursuing a doctoral degree at a Canadian university. To be eligible, students must have achieved a first-class average in their previous two years of study and must be nominated by their university. The deadline for application is November 1, and more information can be found at [https://www.vanier.gc.ca](https://www.vanier.gc.ca).
### Canada Graduate Scholarships-Master's Program
The Canada Graduate Scholarships-Master's Program provides $17,500 for one year to students pursuing a master's degree at a Canadian university. To be eligible, students must have achieved a first-class average in their previous two years of study and must be nominated by their university. The deadline for application is December 1, and more information can be found at [https://www.nserc-crsng.gc.ca](https://www.nserc-crsng.gc.ca).
### Ontario Trillium Scholarships
The Ontario Trillium Scholarships provide $40,000 per year for four years to international students pursuing a doctoral degree at an Ontario university. To be eligible, students must have achieved a first-class average in their previous two years of study and must be nominated by their university. The deadline for application is March 1, and more information can be found at [https://www.ouac.on.ca](https://www.ouac.on.ca).
### Quebec Merit Scholarships for Foreign Students
The Quebec Merit Scholarships for Foreign Students provide $25,000 per year for three years to international students pursuing a doctoral degree at a Quebec university. To be eligible, students must have achieved a first-class average in their previous two years of study and must be nominated by their university. The deadline for application is September 1, and more information can be found at [https://www.frq.gouv.qc.ca](https://www.frq.gouv.qc.ca).
### University of British Columbia Graduate Scholarships
The University of British Columbia Graduate Scholarships provide $20,000 per year for two years to international students pursuing a master's or doctoral degree at the University of British Columbia. To be eligible, students must have achieved a first-class average in their previous two years of study and must be nominated by their university. ## Application Steps
To apply for Canada graduate scholarships, international students, including asylum seekers, must follow these steps:
1. Check their eligibility for the scholarship program.
2. Choose a Canadian university and program that is eligible for the scholarship.
3. Apply for admission to the university and program.
4. Submit their application for the scholarship through the university's online portal.
5. Provide all required documents, including transcripts and language proficiency test results.
6. Ensure their application is complete and submitted by the deadline.
7. Wait for the university to review their application and make a nomination.
8. Receive notification of the award and accept the scholarship offer.

## Documents Checklist

* Letters of reference from academic or professional supervisors
