# Emergency Funding
The Asylum Seeker Scholarship, offered by the Australian Catholic University, provides up to $25,000 per year to asylum seekers and refugees who hold a valid Bridging Visa E (BVE) or a Temporary Protection Visa (TPV) and are pursuing higher education in Australia.

## Who Qualifies
Asylum seekers in Australia who hold a valid Bridging Visa E (BVE), Temporary Protection Visa (TPV), Safe Haven Enterprise Visa (SHEV), or a Protection Visa (PV) may be eligible for emergency funding. These visa categories allow individuals to live and work in Australia while their asylum claims are being processed. Asylum seekers who are interested in pursuing higher education or vocational training can apply for scholarships and emergency funding to support their studies.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. What is the difference between a BVE and a TPV?
A BVE is a temporary visa that allows asylum seekers to live and work in Australia while their claims are being processed, while a TPV is a temporary visa that provides protection to individuals who are found to be refugees.
2. Can I apply for a scholarship if I am not a refugee?
Yes, some scholarships are available to asylum seekers who are not yet recognized as refugees.
3. How do I apply for a scholarship?

5. Can I apply for multiple scholarships at the same time?
