If you are a migrant student looking to pursue international studies in Germany, the DAAD scholarship is an excellent opportunity for you. The German Academic Exchange Service (DAAD) offers various scholarships to international students, including migrant students, to study in Germany. Who qualifies for the DAAD scholarship? To be eligible, you must be a migrant student with a strong academic record, proficient in German or English, and have a bachelor's degree or equivalent. Here are some DAAD scholarships available to migrant students: 1. Development-Related Postgraduate Courses (EPOS) - covers tuition fees, living expenses, and travel costs, deadline: October 2026; 2. Helmut-Schmidt-Programme (HSP) - covers tuition fees, living expenses, and travel costs, deadline: July 2026; 3. Master's Programmes in German Universities - covers tuition fees and living expenses, deadline: September 2026; 4. PhD Programmes in Germany - covers tuition fees, living expenses, and travel costs, deadline: February 2027; 5. Short-Term Research Grants - covers living expenses and travel costs, deadline: April 2027. To apply for a DAAD scholarship, follow these steps: 1. Choose your scholarship program; 2. Check the eligibility criteria; 3. Prepare your documents, including a passport, birth certificate, diploma, transcripts, language proficiency certificate, and motivation letter; 4. Submit your application online; 5. Wait for the selection process. Make sure to check the following documents: - Passport - Birth certificate - Diploma - Transcripts - Language proficiency certificate - Motivation letter. Frequently asked questions: Q: What is the DAAD scholarship? A: The DAAD scholarship is a scholarship program offered by the German Academic Exchange Service to international students, including migrant students, to study in Germany. Q: Can I apply for a DAAD scholarship if I am not a migrant student? A: No, the DAAD scholarship is specifically designed for migrant students. Q: What is the deadline for applying for a DAAD scholarship? A: The deadline varies depending on the scholarship program, but most deadlines are between July and October 2026. Q: Do I need to speak German to apply for a DAAD scholarship? A: Not always, some programs are taught in English, but German language proficiency is still required for some programs. Q: Can I apply for multiple DAAD scholarships? A: Yes, you can apply for multiple DAAD scholarships, but you can only receive one scholarship at a time. Q: How long does it take to hear back from the DAAD after applying? A: The selection process can take several weeks to several months, depending on the program.
