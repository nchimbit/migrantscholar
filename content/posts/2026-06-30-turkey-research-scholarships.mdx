## Introduction to Turkish Government Research Scholarships
The Turkish Government offers partially funded research scholarships for PhD students to pursue their research studies in Turkish universities. These scholarships are designed to support international students, including those with migrant and refugee backgrounds, to undertake PhD research studies in Turkey. The scholarships provide an opportunity for students to conduct research in a unique and diverse academic environment.

## Who Qualifies
To be eligible for the Turkish Government Research Scholarships, applicants must hold a valid visa category, including:
* Student visa
* Research visa
* Work visa (for part-time research assistantships)
* Refugee visa
* Humanitarian visa
* Other visa categories as specified by the Turkish Government

## Available Scholarships
### 1. Turkey Research Scholarship
The Turkey Research Scholarship provides a coverage amount of up to $1,000 per month, with eligibility limited to students who have completed their master's degree in a relevant field. The deadline for application is November 2026, and more information can be found on the official website: https://www.turkiyeburslari.gov.tr/
### 2. Turkish Education Foundation Scholarship
The Turkish Education Foundation Scholarship offers a coverage amount of up to $750 per month, with eligibility open to students who have been accepted into a PhD program at a Turkish university. The deadline for application is March 2027, and more information can be found on the official website: https://www.turkedunya.com.tr/
### 3. Research Assistant Scholarship
The Research Assistant Scholarship provides a coverage amount of up to $1,200 per month, with eligibility limited to students who have been accepted into a PhD program and have a research assistantship offer from a Turkish university. The deadline for application is November 2026, and more information can be found on the official website: https://www.yok.gov.tr/
### 4. Turkish Government Graduate Scholarship
The Turkish Government Graduate Scholarship offers a coverage amount of up to $900 per month, with eligibility open to students who have completed their bachelor's degree in a relevant field and have been accepted into a PhD program at a Turkish university. The deadline for application is June 2027, and more information can be found on the official website: https://www.turkiye.gov.tr/
### 5. Bosphorus University Scholarship
The Bosphorus University Scholarship provides a coverage amount of up to $1,500 per month, with eligibility limited to students who have been accepted into a PhD program at Bosphorus University. The deadline for application is November 2026, and more information can be found on the official website: https://www.boun.edu.tr/

## Application Steps
To apply for the Turkish Government Research Scholarships, follow these steps:
1. Check the eligibility criteria for each scholarship
2. Choose the scholarship that best fits your research interests and academic background
3. Prepare the required documents, including transcripts, letters of recommendation, and a research proposal
4. Submit the application through the official website of the scholarship provider
5. Wait for the application to be reviewed and processed
6. Receive the application outcome and follow the instructions for the next steps
7. Apply for a student visa or research visa, if required
8. Register at the university and start the research program

## Documents Checklist
To apply for the Turkish Government Research Scholarships, you will need to provide the following documents:
* Transcripts of previous academic degrees
* Letters of recommendation from academic supervisors or employers
* Research proposal outlining the research topic and objectives
* Proof of language proficiency, if required
* Copy of passport or ID card
* Proof of health insurance, if required
* Other documents as specified by the scholarship provider

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. What is the language of instruction for PhD programs in Turkish universities?
The language of instruction for PhD programs in Turkish universities is Turkish, but some programs may be offered in English or other languages.
2. Can I apply for a Turkish Government Research Scholarship if I have a refugee visa?
Yes, refugees and asylum seekers can apply for the Turkish Government Research Scholarships, but they must meet the eligibility criteria and provide the required documents.
3. How long does it take to process the application for a Turkish Government Research Scholarship?
The application processing time for the Turkish Government Research Scholarships varies, but it can take up to 3-6 months to receive the application outcome.
4. Can I apply for multiple scholarships at the same time?
Yes, you can apply for multiple scholarships, but you must meet the eligibility criteria for each scholarship and provide separate applications.
5. What are the living costs in Turkey for PhD students?
The living costs in Turkey for PhD students vary depending on the location and lifestyle, but a monthly budget of $500-1000 per month is recommended.
6. Can I work part-time while studying for a PhD in Turkey?
Yes, PhD students in Turkey can work part-time, but they must obtain a work permit and comply with the regulations of the Turkish Government.
7. How do I apply for a student visa or research visa for Turkey?
You can apply for a student visa or research visa through the Turkish embassy or consulate in your home country, or online through the official website of the Turkish Government.
8. What are the requirements for the research proposal for the Turkish Government Research Scholarships?
The research proposal should outline the research topic, objectives, methodology, and expected outcomes, and should be written in a clear and concise manner.
