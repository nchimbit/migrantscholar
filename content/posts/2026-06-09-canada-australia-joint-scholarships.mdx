## Introduction to Canada and Australia Joint Scholarships
Migrant students pursuing Master's degrees can now apply for joint scholarships in Canada and Australia, with a focus on **research experience**. These scholarships are available for the academic year 2026-2027 and provide a unique opportunity to study at top universities in Canada and Australia. The joint scholarships are designed to support migrant students in their academic pursuits and provide them with a platform to excel in their chosen fields.

## Who Qualifies
The following categories of migrant students are eligible to apply for the joint scholarships:
* **Permanent Resident** of Canada or Australia
* **Temporary Resident** with a valid study permit
* **Refugee** with a valid study permit
* **International Student** with a valid study permit
* **Protected Person** with a valid study permit
* **Asylum Seeker** with a valid study permit

## Available Scholarships
The following are some of the available joint scholarships for migrant students pursuing Master's degrees in Canada and Australia:
### 1. University of Toronto - Master's Scholarship
The University of Toronto - Master's Scholarship provides **$20,000** per year for two years to migrant students pursuing Master's degrees at the University of Toronto. The scholarship is open to **international students** with a valid study permit. The deadline to apply is **February 1, 2027**. For more information, visit: https://www.utoronto.ca/
### 2. Australian National University - Master's Scholarship
The Australian National University - Master's Scholarship provides **$28,000** per year for two years to migrant students pursuing Master's degrees at the Australian National University. The scholarship is open to **international students** with a valid study permit. The deadline to apply is **April 15, 2027**. For more information, visit: https://www.anu.edu.au/
### 3. McGill University - Master's Scholarship
The McGill University - Master's Scholarship provides **$18,000** per year for two years to migrant students pursuing Master's degrees at McGill University. The scholarship is open to **international students** with a valid study permit. The deadline to apply is **January 15, 2027**. For more information, visit: https://www.mcgill.ca/
### 4. University of Melbourne - Master's Scholarship
The University of Melbourne - Master's Scholarship provides **$25,000** per year for two years to migrant students pursuing Master's degrees at the University of Melbourne. The scholarship is open to **international students** with a valid study permit. The deadline to apply is **May 1, 2027**. For more information, visit: https://www.unimelb.edu.au/
### 5. University of British Columbia - Master's Scholarship
The University of British Columbia - Master's Scholarship provides **$22,000** per year for two years to migrant students pursuing Master's degrees at the University of British Columbia. The scholarship is open to **international students** with a valid study permit. The deadline to apply is **March 1, 2027**. For more information, visit: https://www.ubc.ca/
### 6. Monash University - Master's Scholarship
The Monash University - Master's Scholarship provides **$20,000** per year for two years to migrant students pursuing Master's degrees at Monash University. The scholarship is open to **international students** with a valid study permit. The deadline to apply is **June 1, 2027**. For more information, visit: https://www.monash.edu/

## Application Process
Here is the step-by-step application process for the joint scholarships:
1. **Check eligibility**: Check if you meet the eligibility criteria for the joint scholarships.
2. **Choose a university**: Choose a university in Canada or Australia that offers the joint scholarship.
3. **Review the scholarship criteria**: Review the scholarship criteria and requirements for the chosen university.
4. **Prepare documents**: Prepare all the required documents, including transcripts, **proof of English language proficiency**, and **letters of recommendation**.
5. **Submit an application**: Submit an application to the chosen university, including all the required documents.
6. **Apply for a study permit**: Apply for a **study permit** if you are an international student.
7. **Accept the scholarship offer**: Accept the scholarship offer if you are selected as a recipient.
8. **Enroll in a Master's program**: Enroll in a Master's program at the chosen university.

## Documents Checklist
Here is a checklist of documents required for the joint scholarship application:
* **Transcripts**: Official transcripts from all previous educational institutions.
* **Proof of English language proficiency**: Proof of English language proficiency, such as **IELTS** or **TOEFL**.
* **Letters of recommendation**: At least two letters of recommendation from academic or professional referees.
* **Personal statement**: A personal statement outlining your research interests and goals.
* **CV**: A **CV** outlining your academic and professional experience.
* **Proof of study permit**: Proof of a valid study permit, if applicable.
