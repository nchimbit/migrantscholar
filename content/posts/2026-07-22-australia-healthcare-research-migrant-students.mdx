# Funded Research
The Rhodes Scholarship is available to migrant students in Australia, providing up to AU$70,000 per year for postgraduate studies, and is a great example of the fully funded healthcare research opportunities available. To be eligible, students must hold a valid Australian visa, such as a subclass 500 or 485 visa, and have a strong academic record. For those interested in [fully funded scholarships](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/by-funding/fully-funded), this is a great opportunity to pursue a career in healthcare research.

## Who Qualifies
Migrant students in Australia, including those on a subclass 500 visa (Student Visa), subclass 485 visa (Temporary Graduate Visa), or subclass 820 visa (Partner Visa), may be eligible for fully funded healthcare research opportunities. These scholarships are designed to support students who are passionate about pursuing a career in healthcare research and are committed to making a positive impact in their field. Many [PhD scholarships](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/by-level/phd) are also available for migrant students, providing a great opportunity to advance their research skills.

### Available Scholarships
1. **National Health and Medical Research Council (NHMRC) Scholarship**: Up to AU$114,000 per year, eligible for international students, including migrant students, deadline varies, [https://www.nhmrc.gov.au/funding/research-scholarships](https://www.nhmrc.gov.au/funding/research-scholarships)
2. **Australian Government Research Training Program (RTP) Scholarship**: Up to AU$44,000 per year, eligible for international students, including migrant students, deadline varies, [https://www.education.gov.au/research-training-program](https://www.education.gov.au/research-training-program)
3. **Cancer Council NSW Research Scholarship**: Up to AU$75,000 per year, eligible for international students, including migrant students, deadline varies, [https://www.cancercouncil.com.au/research/research-scholarships/](https://www.cancercouncil.com.au/research/research-scholarships/)
4. **Heart Foundation Australia Research Scholarship**: Up to AU$105,000 per year, eligible for international students, including migrant students, deadline varies, [https://www.heartfoundation.org.au/research/apply-for-research-funding](https://www.heartfoundation.org.au/research/apply-for-research-funding)
5. **Australian Rotary Health Research Scholarship**: Up to AU$30,000 per year, eligible for international students, including migrant students, deadline varies, [https://www.australianrotaryhealth.org.au/apply-for-a-grant/](https://www.australianrotaryhealth.org.au/apply-for-a-grant/)

## Application Steps
1. Check the eligibility criteria for each scholarship to ensure you qualify.
2. Review the application requirements, including any necessary documents or transcripts.
3. Submit your application, including all required documents, before the deadline.
4. Ensure your application is complete and accurate to avoid any delays.
5. Follow up with the scholarship provider to confirm receipt of your application.
6. Prepare for any interviews or assessments that may be required.
7. Notify the scholarship provider of any changes to your application or contact details.
8. Accept the scholarship offer and review the terms and conditions.
