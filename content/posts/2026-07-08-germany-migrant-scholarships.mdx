## DAAD Scholarships for Migrant Students in Germany
The DAAD scholarships are available for migrant students pursuing a Master's degree in Germany, specifically for those holding certain visa categories. 

## Who Qualifies
The following migrant students qualify for the DAAD scholarships:
* Subsidiary protection
* Refugee status
* Deportation ban
* Pending asylum application
* Temporary residence permit
* Humanitarian residence permit
* Toleration
* Other special residence permits for humanitarian reasons

## Available Scholarships
The DAAD offers various scholarships for migrant students, including:
### DAAD Helmut-Schmidt-Programme
The DAAD Helmut-Schmidt-Programme covers a monthly stipend of 850 euros, plus tuition fees and other expenses, with eligibility limited to students from certain countries, and an application deadline of July 31, 2026, and can be found on the official website https://www.daad.de/en/.
### DAAD Development-Related Postgraduate Courses
The DAAD Development-Related Postgraduate Courses scholarship covers a monthly stipend of 750 euros, plus tuition fees and other expenses, with eligibility limited to students from developing countries, and an application deadline of October 31, 2026, and can be found on the official website https://www.daad.de/en/.
### DAAD Public Policy and Good Governance
The DAAD Public Policy and Good Governance scholarship covers a monthly stipend of 850 euros, plus tuition fees and other expenses, with eligibility limited to students from certain countries, and an application deadline of July 31, 2026, and can be found on the official website https://www.daad.de/en/.
### DAAD EPOS
The DAAD EPOS scholarship covers a monthly stipend of 850 euros, plus tuition fees and other expenses, with eligibility limited to students from certain countries, and an application deadline of October 1, 2026, and can be found on the official website https://www.daad.de/en/.
### DAAD Scholarship for Refugees
The DAAD Scholarship for Refugees covers a monthly stipend of 750 euros, plus tuition fees and other expenses, with eligibility limited to refugee students, and an application deadline of November 30, 2026, and can be found on the official website https://www.daad.de/en/.

## Application Steps
To apply for the DAAD scholarships, migrant students must follow these steps:
1. Choose the scholarship and Master's program that best fits their needs and qualifications.
2. Check the eligibility criteria for the chosen scholarship.
3. Prepare all required documents, including academic certificates and language proficiency test results.
4. Fill out the online application form on the DAAD website.
5. Upload all required documents to the online application portal.
6. Submit the application and wait for the confirmation email.
7. Apply for admission to the chosen university and Master's program.
8. Receive the scholarship decision and start the program.

## Documents Checklist
The following documents are required for the DAAD scholarship application:
* Academic certificates and transcripts
* Language proficiency test results (e.g. TOEFL or IELTS)
* Passport copy
* Visa copy (if applicable)
* Proof of refugee status or other special residence permit
* Letter of motivation
* Recommendation letter from a university professor or academic supervisor
