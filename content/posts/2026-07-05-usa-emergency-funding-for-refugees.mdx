## USA Emergency Funding for Refugee Students in Crisis
Refugee students in the USA who hold a valid visa, such as asylum, refugee, or special immigrant visa, can apply for emergency funding through various scholarships to support their education during times of crisis.

## Who Qualifies
To qualify for these emergency funding opportunities, refugee students must hold one of the following visa categories:
* Asylum visa (AS)
* Refugee visa (RE)
* Special Immigrant Visa for Afghan or Iraqi Translators/Interpreters (SQ or SI)
* Special Immigrant Visa for Iraqis or Afghans who worked for the US Government (SIV)
* Refugee admission under the Lautenberg Amendment (LA)
* Conditional Permanent Resident (CPR)
* Temporary Protected Status (TPS)
* Deferred Enforced Departure (DED)

## Available Scholarships
The following scholarships are available to refugee students in crisis:
### The Refugee Education Assistance Program
The Refugee Education Assistance Program provides up to $5,000 in emergency funding to support refugee students' education. To be eligible, students must be enrolled in a US institution and hold a valid refugee or asylum visa. The deadline for application is October 15, 2026. For more information, visit [https://www.brycs.org](https://www.brycs.org).
### The USAHello Scholarship
The USAHello Scholarship offers up to $2,000 in emergency funding to support refugee students' education. To be eligible, students must be enrolled in a US institution and hold a valid refugee or asylum visa. The deadline for application is November 1, 2026. For more information, visit [https://usahello.org](https://usahello.org).
### The International Rescue Committee (IRC) Scholarship
The IRC Scholarship provides up to $3,000 in emergency funding to support refugee students' education. To be eligible, students must be enrolled in a US institution and hold a valid refugee or asylum visa. The deadline for application is December 15, 2026. For more information, visit [https://www.rescue.org](https://www.rescue.org).
### The National Immigration Law Center (NILC) Scholarship
The NILC Scholarship offers up to $1,500 in emergency funding to support refugee students' education. To be eligible, students must be enrolled in a US institution and hold a valid refugee or asylum visa. The deadline for application is January 10, 2027. For more information, visit [https://www.nilc.org](https://www.nilc.org).
### The Immigration and Refugee Services of America (IRSA) Scholarship
The IRSA Scholarship provides up to $2,500 in emergency funding to support refugee students' education. To be eligible, students must be enrolled in a US institution and hold a valid refugee or asylum visa. The deadline for application is March 15, 2027. For more information, visit [https://www.irsa-usa.org](https://www.irsa-usa.org).

## Application Steps
To apply for these emergency funding opportunities, follow these steps:
1. Review the eligibility criteria for each scholarship to determine which ones you qualify for.
2. Gather all required documents, including your visa, proof of enrollment, and proof of financial need.
3. Complete and submit the online application form for each scholarship.
4. Upload all required documents to the online application portal.
5. Pay any required application fees.
6. Submit your application well in advance of the deadline to ensure timely review.
7. Follow up with the scholarship administrator to confirm receipt of your application.
8. Receive notification of the application decision and follow any subsequent instructions.

## Documents Checklist
Make sure to gather the following documents when applying for emergency funding:
* Valid visa (asylum, refugee, or special immigrant visa)
* Proof of enrollment at a US institution
* Proof of financial need (bank statement, tax return, etc.)
* Proof of refugee or asylum status (letter from the US Citizenship and Immigration Services, etc.)
* Transcripts and academic records
* Letters of recommendation
* Personal statement or essay
