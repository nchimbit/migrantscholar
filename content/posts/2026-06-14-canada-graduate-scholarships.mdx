## Introduction to Canada Graduate Scholarships
If you're an international student, migrant student, refugee, or asylum seeker looking to pursue a Master's or PhD program in Canada, you may be wondering what graduate scholarship opportunities are available to you. The good news is that Canada offers various scholarships to international students, including those with asylum seeker status. These scholarships can help cover the cost of tuition fees, living expenses, and other related costs, making it possible for you to achieve your academic goals in Canada.

## Who Qualifies
To be eligible for Canada graduate scholarships, you must meet certain requirements, including holding a valid temporary resident visa or permanent resident visa in one of the following categories:
* Temporary Resident Visa (TRV)
* Study Permit
* Work Permit
* Permanent Resident Visa
* Protected Persons status, including Convention Refugees or persons in need of protection
* Asylum seeker with a pending refugee claim

## Available Scholarships
Here are five real scholarships available to international students, including migrant students, refugees, and asylum seekers:
### Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarships
The Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarships provide $50,000 per year for three years to eligible students pursuing a PhD program in Canada. To be eligible, you must be nominated by a Canadian university, have achieved a first-class average in your previous two years of study, and be pursuing a PhD program in one of the eligible fields. The deadline for application is November 1, 2026. For more information, visit [https://www.vanier.gc.ca](https://www.vanier.gc.ca).
### Canada Graduate Scholarships-Master's Program
The Canada Graduate Scholarships-Master's Program provides $17,500 for one year to eligible students pursuing a Master's program in Canada. To be eligible, you must be enrolled in a Master's program at a Canadian university, have achieved a first-class average in your previous two years of study, and be a citizen or permanent resident of Canada or an international student. The deadline for application is December 1, 2026. For more information, visit [https://www.nserc-crsng.gc.ca](https://www.nserc-crsng.gc.ca).
### Trudeau Foundation Scholarships
The Trudeau Foundation Scholarships provide up to $60,000 per year for three years to eligible students pursuing a PhD program in Canada. To be eligible, you must be pursuing a PhD program in one of the eligible fields, have achieved a first-class average in your previous two years of study, and be committed to contributing to the public good. The deadline for application is January 15, 2027. For more information, visit [https://www.fondationtrudeau.ca](https://www.fondationtrudeau.ca).
### Pierre Elliott Trudeau Foundation Scholarships
The Pierre Elliott Trudeau Foundation Scholarships provide up to $60,000 per year for three years to eligible students pursuing a PhD program in Canada. To be eligible, you must be pursuing a PhD program in one of the eligible fields, have achieved a first-class average in your previous two years of study, and be committed to contributing to the public good. The deadline for application is January 15, 2027. For more information, visit [https://www.fondationtrudeau.ca](https://www.fondationtrudeau.ca).
### Ontario Trillium Scholarships
The Ontario Trillium Scholarships provide $40,000 per year for four years to eligible international students pursuing a PhD program in Ontario. To be eligible, you must be an international student, have achieved a first-class average in your previous two years of study, and be pursuing a PhD program at a university in Ontario. The deadline for application is February 1, 2027. For more information, visit [https://www.ouac.on.ca](https://www.ouac.on.ca).

## Application Steps
To apply for Canada graduate scholarships, follow these steps:
1. Choose a Canadian university and program that you're interested in attending.
2. Review the eligibility criteria for the scholarship you're interested in applying for.
3. Gather all required documents, including transcripts, language proficiency test results, and letters of recommendation.
4. Submit your application to the university and scholarship program.
5. Ensure that you meet the language proficiency requirements for the university and scholarship program.
6. Apply for a study permit and temporary resident visa, if required.
7. Submit your scholarship application, if required.
8. Follow up with the university and scholarship program to ensure that your application has been received and to ask about the status of your application.

## Documents Checklist
Here are some documents that you may need to submit as part of your scholarship application:

* Letters of recommendation from academic or professional references
* Personal statement or research proposal
