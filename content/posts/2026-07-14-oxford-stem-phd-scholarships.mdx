# STEM PhD
The Clarendon Scholarship at University of Oxford is open to international students, including those from [migrant backgrounds](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/by-eligibility/migrants), and provides up to £15,009 towards PhD tuition fees and living expenses.

## Who Qualifies
International students who qualify for a Tier 4 visa, including those with a [family visa](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/visa-categories/family), [work visa](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/visa-categories/work), or [student visa](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/visa-categories/student), are eligible to apply for the STEM PhD programmes at University of Oxford.

### Available Scholarships
* The [Ertegun Graduate Scholarship Programme](https://www.ox.ac.uk/admissions/graduate/fees-and-funding/graduate-scholarships/ertegun-graduate-scholarship-programme) provides up to £15,009 towards PhD tuition fees and living expenses, with eligibility open to international students, deadline 22nd January 2027, and official URL https://www.ox.ac.uk/admissions/graduate/fees-and-funding/graduate-scholarships/ertegun-graduate-scholarship-programme
* The [Oxford-Weidenfeld and Hoffmann Scholarships](https://www.ox.ac.uk/admissions/graduate/fees-and-funding/graduate-scholarships/oxford-weidenfeld-and-hoffmann-scholarships) provides up to £15,009 towards PhD tuition fees and living expenses, with eligibility open to international students from developing countries, deadline 10th December 2026, and official URL https://www.ox.ac.uk/admissions/graduate/fees-and-funding/graduate-scholarships/oxford-weidenfeld-and-hoffmann-scholarships
* The [Commonwealth Scholarships](https://cscuk.fcdo.gov.uk/) provides up to £15,009 towards PhD tuition fees and living expenses, with eligibility open to international students from Commonwealth countries, deadline 1st December 2026, and official URL https://cscuk.fcdo.gov.uk/
* The [Rhodes Scholarships](https://www.rhodeshouse.ox.ac.uk/) provides up to £15,009 towards PhD tuition fees and living expenses, with eligibility open to international students, deadline 1st August 2026, and official URL https://www.rhodeshouse.ox.ac.uk/
* The [Gates Cambridge Scholarship](https://www.gatescambridge.org/) provides up to £15,009 towards PhD tuition fees and living expenses, with eligibility open to international students, deadline 7th December 2026 for US citizens and 3rd January 2027 for international students, and official URL https://www.gatescambridge.org/

## Application Steps
1. Review the eligibility criteria for each [fully funded scholarship](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/by-funding/fully-funded) and select the ones you qualify for.
2. Check the [scholarship deadlines](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/deadlines) for each scholarship and create a timeline for your applications.
3. Prepare your academic documents, including transcripts and certificates, and ensure they meet the requirements for [PhD scholarships](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/by-level/phd).
4. Write a strong personal statement that highlights your academic achievements, research experience, and career goals, and explains why you are applying for a [STEM scholarship](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/by-field/stem).
5. Secure letters of recommendation from academic or professional referees who can speak to your abilities and potential.
6. Submit your applications through the official websites of the scholarships, ensuring you meet all the requirements and deadlines.
7. Prepare for and take any required English language proficiency tests, such as IELTS or TOEFL, and ensure you meet the minimum [English language requirements](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/english-language-requirements) for your chosen university.
8. Apply for a [UK student visa](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/visa-categories/student) once you have received an offer from the University of Oxford.
