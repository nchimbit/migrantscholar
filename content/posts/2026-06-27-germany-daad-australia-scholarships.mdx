## Introduction to DAAD Scholarships for Migrant Students from Australia
If you're a migrant student from Australia looking to pursue higher education in Germany, you may be eligible for the DAAD (Deutscher Akademischer Austauschdienst) scholarships. The DAAD scholarships provide financial support to international students, including those from Australia, to study in Germany. These scholarships offer a unique opportunity for migrant students to experience German culture and academic excellence.

## Who Qualifies for DAAD Scholarships
To be eligible for DAAD scholarships, migrant students from Australia must hold a valid visa. The following visa categories qualify for DAAD scholarships:
* Subclass 500 (Student)
* Subclass 485 (Temporary Graduate)
* Subclass 573 (Higher Education Sector)
* Subclass 574 (Postgraduate Research Sector)
* Subclass 575 (Non-Award Sector)
* Subclass 576 (Foreign Affairs or Defence Sector)

## Available DAAD Scholarships
The DAAD offers a range of scholarships for international students, including migrant students from Australia. Here are five real scholarships available:
### Development-Related Postgraduate Courses
Coverage amount: 750 euros per month
Eligibility: Students from developing countries, including Australia, pursuing a postgraduate degree in a development-related field
Deadline: August 2026

### Helmut-Schmidt-Programme
Coverage amount: 850 euros per month
Eligibility: Students pursuing a master's degree in a field related to public policy, good governance, and civil society

### Konrad-Adenauer-Stiftung Scholarship

Eligibility: Students pursuing a degree in a field related to politics, economics, or law

## Application Steps
Here are the steps to apply for DAAD scholarships:
1. Check the eligibility criteria for the scholarship you're interested in.
2. Prepare the required documents, including your academic transcripts and certificate of English language proficiency.
3. Fill out the application form on the DAAD website.
4. Submit your application, including all required documents, before the deadline.
5. Wait for the selection committee to review your application.
6. If selected, you'll receive an email with instructions on how to accept the scholarship.
7. Apply for a student visa at the German embassy or consulate in Australia.
8. Arrive in Germany and register with the local authorities.
