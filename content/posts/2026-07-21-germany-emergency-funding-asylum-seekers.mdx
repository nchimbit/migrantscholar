# Aid
The Hamburg University Assistance Program exists for asylum seekers and refugees in Germany, providing emergency funding of up to 500 euros per month.

## Who Qualifies
Asylum seekers with a valid residence permit, including those with a visa category D, F, or N, can apply for the Hamburg University Assistance Program. Additionally, refugees with a visa category A, B, or C may also be eligible. The program is designed to support individuals who are pursuing higher education in Germany and are facing financial difficulties.

## Available Scholarships
The following scholarships are available to asylum seekers and refugees in Germany:
### Heinrich Böll Foundation Scholarship
* Amount: 750 euros per month
* Eligibility: Asylum seekers and refugees pursuing a master's or PhD degree
* Deadline: March 1st and September 1st
* Official URL: https://www.boell.de/en/foundation/scholarships
### DAAD Scholarship
* Amount: 750 euros per month
* Eligibility: Asylum seekers and refugees pursuing a master's or PhD degree
* Deadline: Varies depending on the program
* Official URL: https://www.daad.de/en/
### Konrad-Adenauer-Stiftung Scholarship
* Amount: 1000 euros per month
* Eligibility: Asylum seekers and refugees pursuing a master's or PhD degree
* Deadline: January 15th and July 15th
* Official URL: https://www.kas.de/en/
### FriedICH Ebert Stiftung Scholarship
* Amount: 750 euros per month
* Eligibility: Asylum seekers and refugees pursuing a master's or PhD degree
* Deadline: January 10th and July 10th
* Official URL: https://www.fes.de/en/
### Rosa Luxemburg Stiftung Scholarship
* Amount: 1000 euros per month
* Eligibility: Asylum seekers and refugees pursuing a master's or PhD degree
* Deadline: April 1st and October 1st

## Application Process
To apply for the Hamburg University Assistance Program, follow these steps:
1. Check your eligibility for the program by reviewing the visa categories and eligibility criteria.
2. Gather the required documents, including your residence permit, passport, and proof of enrollment at a German university.
3. Fill out the application form, which can be downloaded from the Hamburg University website.
4. Submit the application form and supporting documents to the Hamburg University Assistance Program office.
5. Wait for the application to be processed, which can take up to 6 weeks.
6. If your application is approved, you will receive a notification and instructions on how to receive the emergency funding.
7. Use the emergency funding to support your studies and living expenses in Germany.
8. Renew your application every semester to continue receiving the emergency funding.

## Documents Checklist
When applying for the Hamburg University Assistance Program, make sure to include the following documents:

* Passport
* Proof of enrollment at a German university
* Proof of financial need
