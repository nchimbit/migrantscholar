## Who Qualifies
To be eligible for emergency funding, migrant healthcare professionals must hold a valid US visa, such as an F-1, J-1, H-1B, or O-1 visa. Additionally, they must have a degree in a healthcare-related field and be currently working or pursuing further education in the USA. [Canada scholarships](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/countries/Canada) and [DAAD scholarships](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/universities/daad) also offer opportunities for migrant healthcare professionals.

### Available Scholarships
The following scholarships are available to migrant healthcare professionals in the USA:
1. **The Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowships for New Americans**: Up to $90,000, Eligible for migrant healthcare professionals with a US visa, Deadline: October 27, 2026, [https://pdsoros.org/](https://pdsoros.org/)
2. **The National Health Service Corps Scholarship**: Up to $170,000, Eligible for migrant healthcare professionals with a US visa, pursuing a degree in a primary care field, Deadline: May 2027, [https://nhsc.hrsa.gov/](https://nhsc.hrsa.gov/)
3. **The American Association of Colleges of Nursing Scholarship**: Up to $10,000, Eligible for migrant healthcare professionals with a US visa, pursuing a degree in nursing, Deadline: September 2026, [https://www.aacnnursing.org/](https://www.aacnnursing.org/)
4. **The American Medical Association Foundation Scholarship**: Up to $10,000, Eligible for migrant healthcare professionals with a US visa, pursuing a degree in medicine, Deadline: June 2027, [https://www.ama-assn.org/](https://www.ama-assn.org/)
5. **The Health Resources and Services Administration Scholarship**: Up to $85,000, Eligible for migrant healthcare professionals with a US visa, pursuing a degree in a healthcare-related field, Deadline: March 2027, [https://www.hrsa.gov/](https://www.hrsa.gov/)
