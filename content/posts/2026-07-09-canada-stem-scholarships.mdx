## Canada's Top 5 Scholarships for Migrant Students Pursuing STEM Fields
Migrant students holding a study permit under the Student Direct Stream or other eligible visa categories can apply for various scholarships in Canada, including the ones listed below, which provide up to full tuition coverage and living costs.

## Who Qualifies
To qualify for these scholarships, migrant students must hold one of the following visa categories:
* Study permit under the Student Direct Stream
* Study permit under the regular stream
* Post-graduation work permit
* Open work permit
* Other eligible visa categories as specified by each scholarship provider

## Available Scholarships
### Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarships
The Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarships provide $50,000 per year for three years to top-performing migrant students pursuing a PhD in STEM fields. To be eligible, students must have achieved a first-class average in their previous two years of study and be nominated by their university. The deadline for application is November 2026. For more information, visit https://www.cihr-irsc.gc.ca/e/49373.html.

### NSERC Postgraduate Scholarships
The NSERC Postgraduate Scholarships provide $21,000 per year for two years to migrant students pursuing a master's degree in STEM fields. To be eligible, students must have achieved a first-class average in their previous two years of study and be enrolled in a master's program. The deadline for application is October 2026. For more information, visit https://www.nserc-crsng.gc.ca/Students-Etudiants/PG-CS/index_eng.asp.

### Mitacs Globalink Research Award
The Mitacs Globalink Research Award provides $6,000 to $12,000 for research projects in STEM fields. To be eligible, students must be enrolled in a university outside of Canada and be nominated by their university. The deadline for application is March 2027. For more information, visit https://www.mitacs.ca/en/programs/globalink/globalink-research-award.

### Ontario Trillium Scholarship
The Ontario Trillium Scholarship provides $40,000 per year for four years to top-performing migrant students pursuing a PhD in STEM fields. To be eligible, students must have achieved a first-class average in their previous two years of study and be nominated by their university. The deadline for application is February 2027. For more information, visit https://www.ouac.on.ca/ots/.

### University of Toronto Mastercard Foundation Scholars Program
The University of Toronto Mastercard Foundation Scholars Program provides full tuition coverage and living costs for migrant students pursuing a master's degree in STEM fields. To be eligible, students must have achieved a first-class average in their previous two years of study and be admitted to a master's program at the University of Toronto. The deadline for application is January 2027. For more information, visit https://www.utsc.utoronto.ca/future-students/scholarships/apply-scholarship.

* Language proficiency test results (e.g. IELTS, TOEFL)
