## Introduction to Fully Funded PhD Scholarships in Canada
If you're an international student looking to pursue a PhD in Canada, you're in luck because there are numerous fully funded PhD scholarships available for international students in Canada. These scholarships can cover your tuition fees, living expenses, and other costs associated with studying in Canada. The Canadian government and various universities offer these scholarships to attract top talent from around the world and provide opportunities for international students to pursue their academic goals.

## Who Qualifies for Fully Funded PhD Scholarships in Canada
To be eligible for fully funded PhD scholarships in Canada, you must fall under one of the following visa categories:
* Study permit holder
* Permanent resident
* Protected person
* Convention refugee
* Refugee claimant
* Temporary resident permit holder
* Foreign national with a temporary resident visa

## Available Fully Funded PhD Scholarships in Canada
### Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarships
The Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarships provide $50,000 per year for three years to international students pursuing a PhD in Canada. To be eligible, you must have achieved a first-class average in your previous two years of study and be nominated by a Canadian university. The deadline for application is November 1st, and you can find more information on the [official website](https://vanier.gc.ca/en/home-accueil.html).
### Trudeau Scholarships
The Trudeau Scholarships provide up to $40,000 per year for three years to international students pursuing a PhD in Canada. To be eligible, you must be a citizen of a country other than Canada and be pursuing a PhD in the social sciences or humanities. The deadline for application is December 15th, and you can find more information on the [official website](https://www.trudeaufoundation.ca/en/programs/trudeau-scholarships).
### Banting Postdoctoral Fellowships
The Banting Postdoctoral Fellowships provide $70,000 per year for two years to international students pursuing a postdoctoral fellowship in Canada. To be eligible, you must have completed a PhD within the last five years and be pursuing research in a Canadian university. The deadline for application is October 1st, and you can find more information on the [official website](https://banting.fellowships-bourses.gc.ca/en/home-accueil.html).
### Ontario Trillium Scholarships
The Ontario Trillium Scholarships provide $40,000 per year for four years to international students pursuing a PhD in Ontario, Canada. To be eligible, you must be an international student pursuing a PhD in a Ontario university and be nominated by the university. The deadline for application is March 1st, and you can find more information on the [official website](https://www.ouac.on.ca/ots/).
### Pierre Elliott Trudeau Foundation Scholarship
The Pierre Elliott Trudeau Foundation Scholarship provides up to $40,000 per year for three years to international students pursuing a PhD in Canada. To be eligible, you must be a citizen of a country other than Canada and be pursuing a PhD in the social sciences or humanities. The deadline for application is December 15th, and you can find more information on the [official website](https://www.trudeaufoundation.ca/en/programs/trudeau-scholarships).

## Application Steps for Fully Funded PhD Scholarships in Canada
1. Research and choose a Canadian university that offers your desired PhD program.
2. Check the eligibility criteria for the scholarship you're interested in.
3. Prepare your application package, including your resume, transcripts, and letters of recommendation.
4. Submit your application to the university and the scholarship provider.
5. Wait for the application to be processed and a decision to be made.
6. If you're selected, you'll receive an offer letter and instructions on how to accept the scholarship.
7. Apply for a study permit and other necessary documents to study in Canada.
8. Arrive in Canada and begin your PhD program.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. What is the eligibility criteria for fully funded PhD scholarships in Canada?
The eligibility criteria varies depending on the scholarship provider, but generally includes a high academic standing, language proficiency, and a strong research proposal.
2. How do I apply for a study permit in Canada?
You can apply for a study permit online or through a paper application, and you'll need to provide documentation such as your acceptance letter, proof of financial support, and passport.
3. Can I work while studying in Canada?
Yes, international students can work part-time while studying in Canada, but you'll need to obtain a work permit and follow the regulations set by the Canadian government.
4. How long does it take to process a study permit application?
The processing time for a study permit application varies depending on the country you're applying from, but it can take several weeks or months.
5. Can I bring my family with me to Canada?
Yes, international students can bring their family with them to Canada, but you'll need to demonstrate that you can financially support them and they'll need to obtain the necessary visas.
6. What is the cost of living in Canada?
The cost of living in Canada varies depending on the city and province you're living in, but it's generally higher than in many other countries.
7. Can I stay in Canada after I graduate?
Yes, international students can stay in Canada after they graduate and work, or apply for permanent residency.
8. How do I find accommodation in Canada?
You can find accommodation in Canada by searching online, contacting your university, or working with a real estate agent.
