## Introduction to Fully Funded Scholarships for Refugee Students in the USA
If you are a refugee student seeking to pursue an undergraduate degree in the United States, there are fully funded scholarships available to support your education. These scholarships are designed to help refugee students overcome financial barriers and achieve their academic goals. By applying for these scholarships, refugee students can receive financial support to cover tuition fees, living expenses, and other educational costs.

## Who Qualifies for Refugee Scholarships in the USA
To be eligible for fully funded scholarships for refugee students in the USA, applicants must hold a valid visa category, including:
* Refugee (R-1/R-2)
* Asylee (AS-1/AS-2)
* Special Immigrant Visa (SIV)
* Temporary Protected Status (TPS)
* Deferred Enforced Departure (DED)
*Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA)

## Fully Funded Scholarships for Refugee Students
The following are five fully funded scholarships available to refugee students pursuing undergraduate degrees in the USA:
### Refugee Education Assistance Program (REAP)
The Refugee Education Assistance Program (REAP) provides up to $10,000 per academic year to cover tuition fees, room, and board. To be eligible, applicants must be refugee students admitted to an undergraduate program at a US college or university. The deadline for application is December 15th of each year. For more information, visit: https://www.acf.hhs.gov/orr/programs/reap
### The Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowships for New Americans
The Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowships for New Americans offers up to $90,000 over two years to cover tuition fees and living expenses. To be eligible, applicants must be refugee students who have been granted DACA status or have a green card. The deadline for application is November 1st of each year. For more information, visit: https://www.pdsoros.org/
### The HIAS Scholarship Program
The HIAS Scholarship Program provides up to $5,000 per academic year to cover tuition fees and living expenses. To be eligible, applicants must be refugee students admitted to an undergraduate program at a US college or university. The deadline for application is March 15th of each year. For more information, visit: https://www.hias.org/
### The IIE Scholar Rescue Fund
The IIE Scholar Rescue Fund offers up to $25,000 per academic year to cover tuition fees and living expenses. To be eligible, applicants must be refugee students who are admitted to an undergraduate program at a US college or university and have a proven record of academic excellence. The deadline for application is June 30th of each year. For more information, visit: https://www.scholarrescuefund.org/
### The UNHCR DAFI Scholarship Program
The UNHCR DAFI Scholarship Program provides up to $10,000 per academic year to cover tuition fees, room, and board. To be eligible, applicants must be refugee students admitted to an undergraduate program at a US college or university. The deadline for application is September 15th of each year. For more information, visit: https://www.unhcr.org/

## Application Steps for Refugee Scholarships
To apply for fully funded scholarships for refugee students in the USA, follow these steps:
1. Research and identify scholarship opportunities that match your eligibility criteria.
2. Review the eligibility criteria and application requirements for each scholarship.
3. Prepare the necessary documents, including transcripts, letters of recommendation, and a personal statement.
4. Submit your application by the deadline specified for each scholarship.
5. Wait for the scholarship committee to review your application and make a decision.
6. If selected, receive notification from the scholarship provider and follow the instructions to accept the award.
7. Use the scholarship funds to cover tuition fees, living expenses, and other educational costs.
8. Maintain good academic standing and comply with the terms and conditions of the scholarship.

## Documents Checklist for Refugee Scholarship Applications
The following documents are typically required for refugee scholarship applications:
* Official transcripts from previous academic institutions
* Letters of recommendation from academic or professional mentors
* Personal statement or essay explaining your academic goals and refugee background
* Proof of refugee status, such as a valid visa or asylum approval letter
* Proof of admission to an undergraduate program at a US college or university
* Language proficiency test scores, such as TOEFL or IELTS
* Financial aid application, such as the FAFSA
