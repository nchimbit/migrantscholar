## Introduction to Partially Funded Canada Scholarships for Refugee Students Pursuing Master's Degrees
If you are a refugee student looking to pursue a Master's degree in Canada, there are several partially funded scholarships available to help you achieve your academic goals. The Canadian government and various universities offer these scholarships to support refugee students in their pursuit of higher education. These scholarships can cover a significant portion of your tuition fees and living expenses, making it easier for you to focus on your studies.

## Who Qualifies for These Scholarships
To be eligible for these scholarships, you must fall under one of the following visa categories:
* Protected persons, including Convention refugees
* Persons in need of protection
* Refugees who have been privately sponsored
* Government-assisted refugees
* Refugee claimants with a positive decision on their claim

## Application Steps for Refugee Students
1. Research and choose a university and program that suits your academic goals and interests.
2. Check the eligibility criteria for the scholarship you are applying for.
3. Gather all required documents, including your transcript, language proficiency test results, and refugee status documents.
4. Submit your application for admission to the university.
5. Apply for the scholarship by submitting a separate application form and supporting documents.
6. Wait for the scholarship committee to review your application and make a decision.
7. If you are selected, review and accept the scholarship offer.
8. Begin your studies at the university and ensure you meet all the conditions of the scholarship.

## Documents Checklist for Refugee Students
* Transcript of your undergraduate degree
* Language proficiency test results (e.g. IELTS or TOEFL)
* Refugee status documents (e.g. Protected Person Status Document)
* Passport
* Birth certificate
* Proof of financial support
* Letters of reference
