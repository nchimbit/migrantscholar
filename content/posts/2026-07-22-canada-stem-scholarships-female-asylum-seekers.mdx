# STEM Scholarships
The [Mitacs Globalink](https://www.mitacs.ca/en/programs/globalink) scholarship exists for female asylum seekers in Canada, providing up to $15,000 in funding for STEM research projects. To be eligible, applicants must be in a [Canada scholarships](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/countries/Canada) program, pursuing a degree in a STEM field, and hold a valid [scholarships for refugees](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/by-eligibility/refugees) status.

## Who Qualifies
Female asylum seekers in Canada with a valid visa, such as a [Temporary Resident Visa](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/visit-canada.html) or a [Permanent Resident Visa](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/new-immigrants/new-life-canada.html), may be eligible for these STEM scholarships. Visa categories that qualify include Convention Refugees, [Protected Persons](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/refugees.html), and [Country of Asylum](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/refugees/country-asylum-class.html) claimants.

### Available Scholarships
Here are five fully funded STEM scholarships available to female asylum seekers in Canada:
1. **Mitacs Globalink**: up to $15,000, open to female asylum seekers in Canada pursuing a degree in a STEM field, deadline varies, [https://www.mitacs.ca/en/programs/globalink](https://www.mitacs.ca/en/programs/globalink)
2. **NSERC Postgraduate Scholarships**: up to $21,000, open to female asylum seekers in Canada pursuing a [PhD scholarships](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/by-level/phd) in a STEM field, deadline October 15, [https://www.nserc-crsng.gc.ca/Students-Etudiants/PG-CS/CGSD-BESD_eng.asp](https://www.nserc-crsng.gc.ca/Students-Etudiants/PG-CS/CGSD-BESD_eng.asp)
3. **CIHR Doctoral Research Award**: up to $30,000, open to female asylum seekers in Canada pursuing a [PhD scholarships](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/by-level/phd) in a health-related STEM field, deadline October 1, [https://www.cihr-irsc.gc.ca/e/193.html](https://www.cihr-irsc.gc.ca/e/193.html)
4. **SSHRC Doctoral Fellowships**: up to $20,000, open to female asylum seekers in Canada pursuing a [PhD scholarships](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/by-level/phd) in a social sciences or humanities STEM field, deadline October 17, [https://www.sshrc-crsh.gc.ca/funding-financement/programs-programmes/fellowships/doctoral-eng.aspx](https://www.sshrc-crsh.gc.ca/funding-financement/programs-programmes/fellowships/doctoral-eng.aspx)
5. **Canada Graduate Scholarships**: up to $17,500, open to female asylum seekers in Canada pursuing a [Master's scholarships](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/by-level/masters) in a STEM field, deadline December 1, [https://www.nserc-crsng.gc.ca/Students-Etudiants/PG-CS/CGSM-BESCM_eng.asp](https://www.nserc-crsng.gc.ca/Students-Etudiants/PG-CS/CGSM-BESCM_eng.asp)

## Application Steps
To apply for these STEM scholarships, follow these steps:
1. Review the [scholarship deadlines](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/deadlines) and ensure you meet the eligibility criteria.
2. Choose the scholarship that best fits your field of study and interests.
3. Prepare your application package, including transcripts, letters of recommendation, and a research proposal.
4. Submit your application through the official scholarship website or online portal.
5. Ensure you have a valid [study permit](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/study-canada.html) or [work permit](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/work-canada.html).
6. Follow up with the scholarship provider to confirm receipt of your application.
7. Prepare for potential interviews or assessments as part of the selection process.
8. Review and accept the terms and conditions of the scholarship award.

## Documents Checklist
When applying for STEM scholarships, ensure you have the following documents:
* Valid study permit or work permit
* Official transcripts from previous academic institutions
* Letters of recommendation from academic or professional references
* Research proposal or statement of interest
* Proof of language proficiency (if required)
* Proof of [refugee status](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/by-eligibility/refugees) or [protected person](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/refugees.html) status
