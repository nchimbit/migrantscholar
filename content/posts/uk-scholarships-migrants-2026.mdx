Yes, migrants living in the UK including asylum seekers, refugees, and those with limited leave to remain can apply for UK undergraduate scholarships.

## Who qualifies?

- Asylum seekers with a pending Home Office claim
- Refugees with formal documentation
- Those with humanitarian protection or limited leave to remain
- Forced migrants displaced by conflict or persecution

## Top UK Scholarships for Migrants in 2026

### University of Leeds — Sanctuary Scholarship
**Coverage:** Full tuition fees plus £10,000 per year
**Who can apply:** Forced migrants who cannot access mainstream student finance
**Deadline:** April 2027

### University of Sheffield — Sanctuary Scholarship
**Coverage:** Full tuition fees plus £10,300 per year
**Number of awards:** 5 scholarships per year
**Deadline:** September 2026 entry

### City St George's London — Sanctuary Scholarship
**Coverage:** £17,635 total award
**Who can apply:** Asylum seekers and those with Discretionary Leave to Remain

## Step by Step Application

1. Confirm your immigration status
2. Apply through UCAS to your chosen universities
3. Once you have an offer find the scholarship page
4. Prepare your documents
5. Submit before the deadline

## Documents You Need

- Home Office decision letter or proof of status
- Passport or travel document
- Academic transcripts
- Personal statement
- Two reference letters
- English language proof

## Can undocumented migrants apply?
Very few formal scholarships are open to undocumented migrants. Some university hardship funds do not require immigration documentation.

## Do I need settled status?
No. Many Sanctuary Scholarships are specifically for people without settled status including active asylum seekers.

## Can migrants get full funding?
Yes. Sanctuary Scholarships at Leeds, Sheffield, and City St George's cover full tuition plus a living stipend.
