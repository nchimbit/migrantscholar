## Introduction to Fully Funded Fulbright Scholarships for Asylum Seekers and Refugees
If you are an asylum seeker or refugee looking to pursue your Master's or PhD studies in the USA or Canada, the Fully Funded Fulbright Scholarship is a great opportunity for you. The Fulbright Scholarship covers full tuition fees, living expenses, and other benefits, making it a prestigious and highly sought-after award. As an asylum seeker or refugee, you can apply for this scholarship to advance your education and build a better future for yourself.

## Who Qualifies
To be eligible for the Fully Funded Fulbright Scholarship, you must fall under one of the following visa categories:
* Asylum status (AS)
* Refugee status (RS)
* Temporary Protected Status (TPS)
* Special Immigrant Visa (SIV)
* Deferred Enforced Departure (DED)
* Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA)
* Withholding of Removal (WOR)
* Convention Against Torture (CAT)

## Available Scholarships
The following scholarships are available for asylum seekers and refugees pursuing Master's and PhD studies in the USA and Canada:
### Fulbright Foreign Student Program
The Fulbright Foreign Student Program provides full funding for international students, including asylum seekers and refugees, to pursue their Master's or PhD studies in the USA. The coverage amount is up to $50,000 per year, and the eligibility criteria include a bachelor's degree, English proficiency, and a strong academic record. The deadline for application is October 15th every year, and the official website is https://fulbright.amideast.org/.
### Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarships
The Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarships provide $50,000 per year for three years to international students, including asylum seekers and refugees, pursuing their PhD studies in Canada. The eligibility criteria include a master's degree, English or French proficiency, and a strong academic record. The deadline for application is November 1st every year, and the official website is https://www.cihr-irsc.gc.ca/.
### Trudeau Scholarships
The Trudeau Scholarships provide up to $40,000 per year for three years to international students, including asylum seekers and refugees, pursuing their PhD studies in Canada. The eligibility criteria include a master's degree, English or French proficiency, and a strong academic record. The deadline for application is December 15th every year, and the official website is https://www.trudeaufoundation.ca/.
### DAAD Scholarship for Refugees
The DAAD Scholarship for Refugees provides up to €1,200 per month for international students, including asylum seekers and refugees, pursuing their Master's or PhD studies in the USA or Canada. The eligibility criteria include a bachelor's or master's degree, English or German proficiency, and a strong academic record. The deadline for application is January 15th every year, and the official website is https://www.daad.de/.
### Ford Foundation Fellowship Program
The Ford Foundation Fellowship Program provides up to $50,000 per year for international students, including asylum seekers and refugees, pursuing their PhD studies in the USA. The eligibility criteria include a master's degree, English proficiency, and a strong academic record. The deadline for application is December 15th every year, and the official website is https://sites.nationalacademies.org/.
