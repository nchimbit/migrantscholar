## Introduction to Germany's DAAD Helmut Schmidt Programme
The DAAD Helmut Schmidt Programme in Germany offers Master's studies in public policy, good governance, and international relations to students from developing countries, including those with migrant and refugee backgrounds. This programme aims to support future leaders by providing them with a high-quality education and skills to address global challenges. By studying in Germany, students can gain valuable knowledge, experience, and networks that can help them make a positive impact in their home countries.

## Programme Details
The programme is designed for students who are interested in pursuing a Master's degree in public policy, good governance, and international relations. It is a two-year programme that covers a wide range of topics, including political science, economics, law, and social sciences.

### Who Qualifies
To qualify for the programme, students must meet the following eligibility criteria:
* Hold a Bachelor's degree in a relevant field
* Be a citizen of a developing country
* Have at least two years of work experience
* Meet the language requirements (English or German)
* Hold a valid visa, including:
  * Visa for study purposes (Visum zum Studienzahl)
  * Visa for language courses (Visum für Sprachkurse)
  * Visa for vocational training (Visum für Berufsausbildung)
  * Visa for research purposes (Visum zu Forschungszwecken)
  * Visa for internship purposes (Visum für Praktika)
