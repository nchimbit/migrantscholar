## Introduction to DAAD Fully Funded Scholarships in Germany
If you're an international student looking to study in Germany, you're in luck because the German Academic Exchange Service (DAAD) offers fully funded scholarships to international students, including migrant students, refugees, and asylum seekers. These scholarships provide a unique opportunity for students to pursue their academic goals in a renowned German university, with all expenses covered. The DAAD scholarships are available for various fields of study, including engineering, humanities, social sciences, and natural sciences.

## Who Qualifies for DAAD Scholarships
To be eligible for DAAD scholarships, you must belong to one of the following visa categories:
* Subsidiary protection
* Refugee status
* Asylum seeker
* Migrant student with a valid residence permit
* International student from a developing country
The DAAD scholarships are highly competitive, and the selection process is based on academic merit, language proficiency, and personal statement.

## Available Scholarships
### 1. Development-Related Postgraduate Courses (DRP) Scholarship
The DRP scholarship is a fully funded scholarship that covers tuition fees, living expenses, and travel costs, with a total coverage amount of 750 euros per month. ## Application Steps
1. Check your eligibility for the scholarship by reviewing the visa categories and academic requirements.
2. Choose the scholarship program that best fits your academic goals and interests.
3. Review the application deadline and make sure you have enough time to prepare your documents.
4. Prepare your academic transcripts, language proficiency certificates, and personal statement.
5. Submit your application through the official website or by email, depending on the scholarship program.
6. Wait for the selection process to be completed, which may take several weeks or months.
7. If selected, you will receive a notification with instructions on how to proceed with the next steps.
8. Prepare for your departure to Germany by obtaining a visa, booking flights, and arranging accommodation.
