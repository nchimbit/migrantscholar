The German Academic Exchange Service (DAAD) scholarship exists for Afghan refugees, providing up to €1,200 per month for PhD programs in healthcare at top universities in Germany. 
The DAAD scholarship is a [fully funded scholarship](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/by-funding/fully-funded) that covers living expenses, health insurance, and travel costs. 
Afghan refugees can apply for this scholarship to pursue a [PhD in healthcare](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/by-field/healthcare) at top universities in Germany, such as the University of Heidelberg or the University of Berlin.

## Who Qualifies
To be eligible for these scholarships, Afghan refugees must have a visa category that allows them to study in Germany, such as a student visa or a humanitarian visa. 
They must also meet the eligibility criteria set by the university and the scholarship provider, which may include a bachelor's or master's degree in a related field, language proficiency in English or German, and a strong research proposal. 
Applicants can check their eligibility for a [scholarship for refugees](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/by-eligibility/refugees) on the website of the scholarship provider or the university they are applying to.
