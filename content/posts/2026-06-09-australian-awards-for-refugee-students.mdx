Yes — refugee students in Australia can access fully funded scholarships that cover tuition fees, living expenses, and travel costs. Eligibility depends on your visa status and which university or government body administers the award. This guide covers the top verified awards open to refugees and asylum seekers in 2026, with deadlines and application steps.

## Who qualifies for Australian refugee scholarships?

You may qualify if you fall into one of these categories:

- **Protection visa holder** — you have been granted a temporary or permanent protection visa by the Australian government
- **Asylum seeker** — your protection visa application is pending with the Department of Home Affairs
- **Humanitarian visa holder** — you hold a visa under Australia's Humanitarian Programme (subclasses 200, 201, 202, 203, 204)
- **Bridging visa holder** — you are awaiting a visa decision and are currently on a bridging visa
- **Refugee resettled in Australia** — you arrived through the UNHCR referral process

> **Important:** Each scholarship has its own eligibility rules. Always check the specific requirements before applying — some require you to already be enrolled at an Australian university, while others accept applications from offshore.

## Top Fully Funded Australian Scholarships for Refugees in 2026

### 1. Australia Awards Scholarships
**Coverage:** Full tuition fees + living allowance + return airfare + health insurance
**Who can apply:** Citizens of eligible developing countries, including refugees referred through UNHCR
**Study level:** Undergraduate and postgraduate
**Deadline:** 30 April 2027 (for 2028 intake)
**Official link:** [australiaawards.gov.au](https://www.australiaawards.gov.au)

Australia Awards is the Australian government's flagship international scholarship programme. It is one of the most comprehensive fully funded awards available, covering every major cost of study.

---

### 2. Refugee Education Scholarship (RES)
**Coverage:** $10,000 per year for up to three years
**Who can apply:** Refugees and asylum seekers currently living in Australia
**Study level:** Undergraduate
**Deadline:** 15 August 2026
**Note:** Must be enrolled or applying to an Australian university

This award is specifically designed for displaced students already living in Australia who cannot access standard government student loans (HECS-HELP) due to their visa status.

---

### 3. Humanitarian Scholarship
**Coverage:** Full tuition fee waiver + $20,000 per year stipend
**Who can apply:** Students holding humanitarian or protection visas
**Study level:** Postgraduate
**Deadline:** 31 October 2026

This award targets postgraduate students and is one of the highest-value stipend awards available to humanitarian visa holders in Australia.

### 4. University of Melbourne — Refugee Scholarship
**Coverage:** Full tuition fee waiver + $30,000 per year stipend
**Who can apply:** Refugees and asylum seekers accepted into a University of Melbourne programme
**Study level:** Undergraduate and postgraduate
**Deadline:** 30 November 2026
**Official link:** [unimelb.edu.au/scholarships](https://scholarships.unimelb.edu.au)

The University of Melbourne offers one of the most generous refugee-specific awards in the country. The $30,000 annual stipend covers accommodation, food, and daily living costs comfortably.

### 5. Australian Catholic University — Refugee Scholarship
**Coverage:** Full tuition fee waiver + $25,000 per year stipend
**Who can apply:** Asylum seekers and refugees enrolled at ACU
**Study level:** Undergraduate and postgraduate

ACU has a dedicated refugee support programme with wraparound services including academic mentoring, counselling, and community support alongside the financial award.

## Step-by-step application process

**Step 1: Confirm your visa status**
Check which category you fall under — protection visa, bridging visa, or humanitarian visa. This determines which scholarships you are eligible for.

**Step 2: Choose your course and university**
Search for courses you want to study and shortlist universities that offer refugee-specific scholarships. Many awards require you to have an offer of admission first.

**Step 3: Apply to your chosen university**
Submit your application directly to the university. Include a note that you are applying as a refugee or asylum seeker — most universities have a dedicated admissions pathway.

**Step 4: Find the scholarship application form**
Once you have an offer, go to the university's scholarships page and search for refugee or humanitarian awards. Each has its own separate application form.

**Step 5: Prepare your documents**
Gather everything before deadlines hit — most scholarships close months before the academic year starts.

**Step 6: Write your personal statement**
Be honest about your background and journey. Scholarship panels look for academic potential, resilience, and a clear plan for what you will do after your studies.

**Step 7: Submit and follow up**
Submit before the deadline and keep a copy of everything. Follow up with the scholarships office if you have not heard back within six weeks.

## Documents checklist

Prepare these documents before you apply:

- [ ] Protection visa, bridging visa, or proof of your asylum application
- [ ] Passport or travel document
- [ ] Academic transcripts from previous study
- [ ] English language test results — IELTS, TOEFL, or PTE (check if required for your scholarship)
- [ ] Personal statement — explain your background, goals, and why you need this award
- [ ] Two reference letters — from a teacher, community leader, or support worker
- [ ] Proof of financial need (some scholarships require this)
- [ ] University offer letter (required by most scholarship applications)

## Frequently asked questions

### Can I apply for multiple Australian scholarships at the same time?
Yes. You can apply to as many scholarships as you qualify for simultaneously. However, if you are awarded more than one, you will typically need to choose one — most scholarships cannot be combined.

### Do I need an English language test to apply?
It depends on the scholarship and institution. Some universities waive the English test requirement for refugee applicants who have completed secondary schooling in English. Check each scholarship's specific requirements.

### Can I apply if my protection visa application is still pending?
Yes. Several scholarships — including the Refugee Education Scholarship — accept applications from asylum seekers whose cases are still being processed. You will need to provide proof of your pending application.

### Can I apply from outside Australia?
Most refugee-specific university scholarships require you to already be in Australia. The Australia Awards Scholarships is the main option for applicants based outside the country.

### How long does it take to get a decision?
Processing times vary widely — from six weeks to six months depending on the scholarship. Apply as early as possible and do not wait until the deadline.

### What if I am not selected?
You can reapply in the next round. Ask the scholarships office for feedback on your application. Consider applying to additional universities that offer similar awards — over 40 Australian universities have some form of refugee scholarship or hardship fund.

### Can I work while on a refugee scholarship?
Yes. Most protection and humanitarian visa holders in Australia have the right to work. Scholarship conditions vary — some have restrictions on paid employment, so check the terms of your specific award.

### Is there support beyond the scholarship funding?
Yes. Universities like ACU and Melbourne offer wraparound support including academic mentoring, mental health counselling, housing assistance, and peer support networks specifically for refugee students.
