## Introduction to DAAD Fully Funded Scholarships for Asylum Seekers in Germany
The German Academic Exchange Service (DAAD) offers fully funded scholarships to asylum seekers in Germany, providing them with the opportunity to pursue higher education in the country. These scholarships are designed to support master's and PhD students who are seeking asylum in Germany, helping them to achieve their academic goals and build a better future. As of 2026, the DAAD has introduced several new scholarship programs specifically for asylum seekers, making it an exciting time for those looking to further their education.

## Who Qualifies for DAAD Scholarships
To be eligible for DAAD scholarships, asylum seekers must hold a specific visa category, including:
* Subsidiary protection status (§ 4 Abs. 1 S. 2 AufenthG)
* Refugee status (§ 3 Abs. 1 AsylG)
* Temporary permission to stay (§ 55 Abs. 1 AsylG)
* Deportation ban (§ 60 Abs. 5 AufenthG)
* Residence permit for humanitarian reasons (§ 24 Abs. 1 AufenthG)

## Available Scholarships
### Development-Related Postgraduate Courses (EPOS)
The Development-Related Postgraduate Courses (EPOS) scholarship provides a monthly stipend of 750 euros, as well as a one-time payment of 1,500 euros for travel and other expenses. To be eligible, applicants must hold a bachelor's degree and have at least two years of work experience. The deadline for application is August 31, 2026, and more information can be found at https://www.daad.de/en/.
