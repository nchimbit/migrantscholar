The Refugee Scholarship exists for international students with refugee backgrounds, providing up to $50,000 in funding to pursue higher education in the USA. 

## Overview
The USA provides various scholarship opportunities for international students with refugee backgrounds, aiming to support their higher education and career goals. These scholarships not only offer financial assistance but also create a pathway for refugees to rebuild their lives and contribute to their communities.

## Who Qualifies
International students with refugee backgrounds who hold specific visa categories are eligible to apply for these scholarships. The qualifying visa categories include:
* F-1 visa
* J-1 visa
* F-2 visa
* J-2 visa
* Asylee (Granted Asylum)
* Refugee (Granted Refugee Status)
* Temporary Protected Status (TPS)
* Deferred Enforced Departure (DED)

## Available Scholarships
### The Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowships for New Americans
The Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowships for New Americans provide $90,000 in funding over two years to support graduate studies. Eligible applicants must be 30 years old or younger and hold a bachelor's degree. The deadline for application is November 1, 2026. For more information, visit https://www.pdsoros.org.

### The Fulbright Program
The Fulbright Program offers scholarships for international students to pursue higher education in the USA, with funding amounts varying based on the program. Eligible applicants must hold a bachelor's degree and have a strong academic record. The deadline for application is October 11, 2026. For more information, visit https://fulbright.americancouncils.org.

### The Hubert H. Humphrey Fellowship Program
The Hubert H. Humphrey Fellowship Program provides $50,000 in funding for one year to support non-degree graduate studies. Eligible applicants must hold a bachelor's degree, have a minimum of five years of work experience, and be under 45 years old. The deadline for application is October 1, 2026. For more information, visit https://www.humphreyfellowship.org.

### The Margaret McNamara Memorial Fund
The Margaret McNamara Memorial Fund offers scholarships ranging from $12,000 to $15,000 to support women from developing countries in their higher education. Eligible applicants must be 25 years old or older, hold a bachelor's degree, and be enrolled in a graduate program. The deadline for application is September 15, 2026. For more information, visit https://www.mmfund.org.

### The AAUW International Fellowship
The AAUW International Fellowship provides $20,000 to $50,000 in funding to support women from outside the USA in their graduate studies. Eligible applicants must hold a bachelor's degree, have a strong academic record, and be committed to improving the lives of women and girls. The deadline for application is November 15, 2026. For more information, visit https://www.aauw.org.

## Application Steps
