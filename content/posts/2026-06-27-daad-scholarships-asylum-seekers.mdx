## Introduction to DAAD Scholarships for Asylum Seekers
The German Academic Exchange Service (DAAD) offers partially funded scholarships to asylum seekers who wish to pursue postgraduate studies in Germany. These scholarships aim to support asylum seekers in completing their higher education and integrating into German society. By providing financial assistance, DAAD enables asylum seekers to focus on their academic pursuits and build a better future for themselves.

## Who Qualifies for DAAD Scholarships
To be eligible for DAAD scholarships, asylum seekers must belong to specific visa categories, including:
* Subsidiary protection
* Refugee status
* Asylum seeker with a temporary residence permit
* Asylum seeker with a tolerated stay permit
* Asylum seeker with a pending asylum application
* Asylum seeker with a recognized refugee status

## Application Steps
To apply for DAAD scholarships, follow these steps:
1. Check your eligibility for the scholarship program
2. Choose your desired course of study and university
3. Prepare all required documents, including academic transcripts and language proficiency certificates
4. Fill out the online application form on the DAAD website
5. Submit your application before the deadline
6. Wait for the selection committee's decision
7. Receive a confirmation email and start preparing for your studies in Germany
8. Arrive in Germany and begin your postgraduate program

## Documents Checklist
To apply for DAAD scholarships, you will need to provide the following documents:
* Academic transcripts and diplomas
* Language proficiency certificates (German or English)
* Passport and visa documents
* Proof of financial support
* Letter of motivation and personal statement
* References from academic or professional supervisors
* Proof of health insurance

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. What is the deadline for applying to DAAD scholarships for asylum seekers?
The deadline for applying to DAAD scholarships for asylum seekers varies depending on the scholarship program, but most deadlines fall in October or November 2026.

Yes, you can apply for a DAAD scholarship if you have a pending asylum application, but you must provide proof of your application and any relevant documentation.
