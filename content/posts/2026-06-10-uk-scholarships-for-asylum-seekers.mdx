## Introduction to Fully Funded UK Scholarships for Asylum Seekers and Refugees
If you are an asylum seeker or refugee looking to pursue higher education in the UK, there are several fully funded scholarships available to support your academic endeavors. These scholarships are designed to help individuals who have been displaced due to conflict, persecution, or natural disasters to access quality education and rebuild their lives. By applying for these scholarships, you can receive financial support to cover your tuition fees, living expenses, and other study-related costs.

## Who Qualifies for Fully Funded UK Scholarships
To be eligible for fully funded UK scholarships, you must hold a specific visa category, including:
* Refugee status
* Humanitarian protection
* Discretionary leave
* Leave outside the rules
* Asylum seeker status
* Section 67 leave
* Section 4(1) leave

## Application Steps
1. Check your eligibility for the scholarship by reviewing the visa categories and academic requirements.
2. Choose the scholarship you wish to apply for and review the application process.
3. Prepare your application documents, including your passport, visa, and academic transcripts.
4. Submit your application online or by post, depending on the scholarship requirements.
5. Receive an acknowledgement of your application and wait for the selection process to be completed.
6. If selected, receive an offer letter and accept the scholarship by the deadline.
7. Complete any additional requirements, such as language proficiency tests or medical checks.
8. Begin your studies at the start of the academic year, using the scholarship funding to support your education.

## Frequently Asked Questions
What is the difference between a refugee and an asylum seeker?
A refugee is someone who has been granted protection in the UK due to a fear of persecution in their home country, while an asylum seeker is someone who has applied for protection but has not yet received a decision. Both refugees and asylum seekers may be eligible for fully funded UK scholarships.
Can I apply for a scholarship if I am still waiting for my asylum claim to be processed?
Yes, you can apply for a scholarship while your asylum claim is being processed, but you must have received a decision on your claim by the time the scholarship is awarded.
What is the deadline for applying for a fully funded UK scholarship?
The deadline for applying for a fully funded UK scholarship varies depending on the scholarship, but it is usually between January and May of each year.
Do I need to have a certain level of English language proficiency to apply for a scholarship?
Yes, most scholarships require a certain level of English language proficiency, such as IELTS or TOEFL, to ensure that you can succeed in your studies.
Can I apply for more than one scholarship at the same time?
Yes, you can apply for more than one scholarship at the same time, but you must meet the eligibility criteria for each scholarship and submit a separate application for each one.
What happens if I am awarded a scholarship but my asylum claim is refused?
If you are awarded a scholarship but your asylum claim is refused, you may still be able to continue your studies using the scholarship funding, but you will need to discuss your situation with the scholarship provider and the university.
Can I apply for a scholarship if I have already started my studies in the UK?
Yes, you can apply for a scholarship if you have already started your studies in the UK, but you must meet the eligibility criteria and submit a new application for the scholarship.
What is the amount of funding provided by a fully funded UK scholarship?
The amount of funding provided by a fully funded UK scholarship varies depending on the scholarship, but it can include full tuition fee waivers and a stipend of up to £20,000 per annum.
