## Partially Funded PhD Scholarships in Germany for Asylum Seekers
Asylum seekers are eligible to apply for partially funded PhD scholarships in STEM fields in Germany, enabling them to pursue their academic goals while rebuilding their lives. 
### Who Qualifies
To be eligible for these scholarships, asylum seekers must hold one of the following visa categories:
* Subsidiary protection
* Refugee status
* Asylum seeker with a temporary residence permit
* Humanitarian residence permit
* Other temporary permits that grant access to the German education system

## Available Scholarships
Here are five partially funded PhD scholarships in STEM fields available to asylum seekers in Germany:
### 1. DAAD Scholarship
The DAAD Scholarship offers coverage of up to €1,200 per month for living costs, plus tuition fees. Eligible candidates must hold a master's degree in a STEM field and have a strong academic record. The deadline for applications is November 2026. For more information, visit https://www.daad.de.
### 2. Heinrich Böll Foundation Scholarship
The Heinrich Böll Foundation Scholarship provides coverage of up to €1,500 per month for living costs, plus tuition fees. Eligible candidates must hold a master's degree in a STEM field and be committed to social and political engagement. The deadline for applications is June 2027. For more information, visit https://www.boell.de.
### 3. Friedrich Ebert Foundation Scholarship
The Friedrich Ebert Foundation Scholarship offers coverage of up to €1,200 per month for living costs, plus tuition fees. Eligible candidates must hold a master's degree in a STEM field and be committed to social democracy. The deadline for applications is March 2027. For more information, visit https://www.fes.de.
### 4. Konrad-Adenauer-Stiftung Scholarship
The Konrad-Adenauer-Stiftung Scholarship provides coverage of up to €1,500 per month for living costs, plus tuition fees. Eligible candidates must hold a master's degree in a STEM field and be committed to Christian democratic values. The deadline for applications is September 2026. For more information, visit https://www.kas.de.
### 5. Hans Böckler Foundation Scholarship
The Hans Böckler Foundation Scholarship offers coverage of up to €1,200 per month for living costs, plus tuition fees. Eligible candidates must hold a master's degree in a STEM field and be committed to trade unionism. The deadline for applications is January 2027. For more information, visit https://www.boeckler.de.

## Application Process
To apply for these scholarships, follow these steps:
1. Choose the scholarship that best fits your academic and personal goals.
2. Review the eligibility criteria and ensure you meet all the requirements.
3. Prepare all the necessary documents, including your academic transcripts and proof of language proficiency.
4. Submit your application before the deadline, either online or by post.
5. Wait for the selection committee to review your application and make a decision.
6. If selected, you will be notified and required to confirm your acceptance of the scholarship.
7. Apply for a student visa, if necessary, and make travel arrangements to Germany.
8. Register at your chosen university and begin your PhD program.

## Documents Checklist
When applying for these scholarships, make sure to include the following documents:
* Academic transcripts and diplomas
* Proof of language proficiency (German or English)
* Letter of motivation and personal statement
* Reference letters from academic or professional supervisors
* Proof of visa status and residency permit
* Proof of financial need (if applicable)
