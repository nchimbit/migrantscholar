## Introduction to Migrant Student Awards in the USA
If you are a migrant student in the USA, you may be eligible for various scholarships and awards to support your education. These scholarships are designed to help migrant students achieve their academic goals and pursue their dreams. To get started, it's essential to understand the eligibility criteria and application process for these awards.

## Who Qualifies for Migrant Student Awards
To qualify for migrant student awards, you must hold a valid visa that allows you to study in the USA. The following visa categories are typically eligible:
* F-1 visa: Academic Student
* F-2 visa: Dependents of F-1 visa holders
* J-1 visa: Exchange Visitor
* J-2 visa: Dependents of J-1 visa holders
* DACA (Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals)
* TPS (Temporary Protected Status)
* Asylum or refugee status

## Available Scholarships for Migrant Students
There are numerous scholarships available for migrant students in the USA. Here are five examples:
### TheDream.US Scholarship
TheDream.US Scholarship offers up to $29,000 per year for tuition and fees. To be eligible, you must be a DACA or TPS recipient, have a minimum GPA of 2.5, and be enrolled in an associate's or bachelor's degree program. The deadline for application is February 27, 2026. For more information, visit [https://thedream.us/](https://thedream.us/).
### Golden Door Scholars
Golden Door Scholars offers up to $40,000 per year for tuition and fees. To be eligible, you must be a DACA or TPS recipient, have a minimum GPA of 3.0, and be enrolled in a bachelor's degree program. The deadline for application is January 15, 2026. For more information, visit [https://www.goldendoorscholars.org/](https://www.goldendoorscholars.org/).
### Hispanic Scholarship Fund
Hispanic Scholarship Fund offers up to $5,000 per year for tuition and fees. To be eligible, you must be of Hispanic descent, have a minimum GPA of 3.0, and be enrolled in a bachelor's degree program. The deadline for application is February 15, 2026. For more information, visit [https://www.hsf.net/](https://www.hsf.net/).
### Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowships for New Americans
Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowships for New Americans offers up to $90,000 over two years for graduate studies. To be eligible, you must be a migrant student who is 30 years old or younger, have a bachelor's degree, and be enrolled in a graduate degree program. The deadline for application is November 1, 2026. For more information, visit [https://www.pdsoros.org/](https://www.pdsoros.org/).
### National Immigration Law Center Scholarship
National Immigration Law Center Scholarship offers up to $2,500 per year for tuition and fees. To be eligible, you must be a migrant student who is enrolled in a bachelor's degree program, have a minimum GPA of 2.5, and demonstrate financial need. The deadline for application is March 15, 2026. For more information, visit [https://www.nilc.org/](https://www.nilc.org/).

## Application Steps for Migrant Student Awards
To apply for migrant student awards, follow these steps:
1. Research and identify the scholarships you are eligible for.
2. Review the eligibility criteria and application requirements for each scholarship.
3. Gather all required documents, including transcripts, letters of recommendation, and proof of visa status.
4. Submit your application before the deadline.
5. Ensure that your application is complete and accurate.
6. Follow up with the scholarship provider to confirm receipt of your application.
7. Prepare for interviews or additional evaluations, if required.
8. Receive notification of the scholarship decision and follow the instructions for claiming your award.

## Documents Checklist for Migrant Student Awards
To apply for migrant student awards, you will need to provide the following documents:
* Proof of visa status (F-1, F-2, J-1, J-2, DACA, TPS, asylum, or refugee status)
* Passport
* I-20 form (for F-1 visa holders)
* DS-2019 form (for J-1 visa holders)
* Transcripts
* Letters of recommendation
* Proof of financial need (if required)
* Proof of enrollment in a degree program
