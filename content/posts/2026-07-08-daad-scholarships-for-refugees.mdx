## Germany's DAAD Scholarships for Refugee Students
The DAAD Scholarships for Refugee Students are available for refugee students pursuing undergraduate or graduate degrees in Germany, providing them with the opportunity to study at a German university and gain a high-quality education.

## Who Qualifies
To be eligible for the DAAD Scholarships for Refugee Students, applicants must hold a valid visa in one of the following categories:
* Subsidiary protection
* Asylum
* Refugee status
* Temporary residence permit with a valid asylum application
* Temporary suspension of deportation
* Deportation ban
* Other humanitarian residence permits

## Available Scholarships
### Development-Related Postgraduate Courses (DRP)
The Development-Related Postgraduate Courses (DRP) scholarship covers €750 per month for living expenses, plus tuition fees and other costs, and is eligible for students from developing countries, including refugees, who are pursuing a master's or doctoral degree in a development-related field. The deadline for application is usually in November of each year, and more information can be found on the official website: https://www.daad.de.

## Application Steps
1. Choose the scholarship and the university that best fits your academic goals and background.
2. Review the eligibility criteria and ensure that you meet all the requirements.
3. Prepare all the necessary documents, including your academic transcripts, language proficiency certificates, and visa documentation.
4. Submit your application through the DAAD online application portal.
5. Wait for the application to be processed and reviewed by the DAAD and the university.
6. If your application is successful, you will receive a letter of acceptance and a scholarship award letter.
7. Use the scholarship award letter to apply for a visa at the German embassy or consulate in your home country.
8. Once you have received your visa, you can travel to Germany and begin your studies.
