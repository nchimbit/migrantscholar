## Introduction to Partial Funding Opportunities for Migrant Students in Turkey
As a migrant student in Turkey, you may be wondering if there are partial funding opportunities available to support your undergraduate studies in business and economics. The good news is that yes, there are several opportunities that can help you cover some of the costs associated with studying in Turkey. In this article, we will explore some of the partial funding opportunities available to migrant students in Turkey, with a focus on undergraduate studies in business and economics.

## Who Qualifies
To be eligible for partial funding opportunities in Turkey, migrant students must hold a valid residence permit and fall under one of the following visa categories:
* Student visa (Öğrenci Vizesi)
* Family residence permit (Aile İkamet İzni)
* Humanitarian residence permit (İnsani İkamet İzni)
* International protection applicant residence permit (Uluslararası Koruma Başvuru Sahibi İkamet İzni)
* Temporary protection identity document (Geçici Koruma Kimlik Belgesi)

## Partial Funding Opportunities
### Turkish Government Scholarship
The Turkish Government Scholarship provides a monthly stipend of 800 TRY to undergraduate students, including those studying business and economics. To be eligible, applicants must be under the age of 25 and have a minimum GPA of 3.0. The deadline for applications is typically in February, and applicants can find more information on the official website: https://www.turkiyeburslari.gov.tr/
### Bilkent University Scholarship
The Bilkent University Scholarship offers a tuition waiver of up to 100% to undergraduate students, including those studying business and economics. To be eligible, applicants must have a minimum GPA of 3.5 and demonstrate financial need. The deadline for applications is typically in June, and applicants can find more information on the official website: https://www.bilkent.edu.tr/
