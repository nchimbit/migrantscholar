The Deutschlandstipendium scholarship exists and qualifies asylum seekers in Germany, providing funding of up to €300 per month for students pursuing higher education.

## Who Qualifies
Asylum seekers with certain visa categories, including those with a residence permit for humanitarian reasons or those with a tolerated stay permit, may be eligible for emergency funding. Specific categories include:
- Subsidiary protection
- Refugee status
- Asylum seekers with a Duldung permit
- Asylum seekers with a residence permit for humanitarian reasons

## Available Scholarships
### DAAD Scholarship
The DAAD Scholarship provides up to €750 per month for international students, including asylum seekers, to pursue higher education in Germany. Eligibility includes a strong academic record and proficiency in German language. Deadline varies depending on the course. Official URL: https://www.daad.de
### Heinrich Böll Foundation Scholarship
The Heinrich Böll Foundation Scholarship offers up to €750 per month for students, including asylum seekers, to pursue higher education in Germany. Eligibility includes a strong academic record, social engagement, and proficiency in German language. Deadline is March 1st and September 1st. Official URL: https://www.boell.de
### Konrad-Adenauer-Stiftung Scholarship
The Konrad-Adenauer-Stifteung Scholarship provides up to €500 per month for international students, including asylum seekers, to pursue higher education in Germany. Eligibility includes a strong academic record, social engagement, and proficiency in German language. Deadline varies depending on the course. Official URL: https://www.kas.de
### Friedrich Ebert Foundation Scholarship
The Friedrich Ebert Foundation Scholarship offers up to €650 per month for students, including asylum seekers, to pursue higher education in Germany. Eligibility includes a strong academic record, social engagement, and proficiency in German language. Deadline is November 30th and May 31st. Official URL: https://www.fes.de
### Rosa Luxemburg Foundation Scholarship
The Rosa Luxemburg Foundation Scholarship provides up to €735 per month for international students, including asylum seekers, to pursue higher education in Germany. Eligibility includes a strong academic record, social engagement, and proficiency in German language. Deadline varies depending on the course. Official URL: https://www.rosalux.de

## Application Steps
1. Check the official website of the scholarship for eligibility criteria and required documents.
2. Prepare a strong application, including a personal statement, CV, and academic transcripts.
3. Submit the application on time, making sure to meet the deadline.
4. Follow up with the scholarship provider to confirm receipt of the application.
5. Prepare for a possible interview, practicing responses to common questions.
6. Wait for the selection committee to review the application and make a decision.
7. Receive notification of the outcome, either an acceptance or rejection letter.
8. Accept the scholarship and enroll in the chosen course, if selected.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. What is the purpose of the emergency funding for asylum seekers?
Emergency funding is designed to support asylum seekers in pursuing higher education in Germany, covering living expenses and tuition fees.
2. Can I apply for multiple scholarships at the same time?
Yes, it is possible to apply for multiple scholarships, but make sure to check the eligibility criteria and deadlines for each one.
3. What are the requirements for proficiency in German language?
A minimum level of B1 in German language is usually required, but this may vary depending on the scholarship and the course.
4. Can I apply for a scholarship if I have a pending asylum application?
Yes, it is possible to apply for a scholarship while the asylum application is being processed, but the applicant must have a valid residence permit.
5. How long does it take to receive the scholarship funding?
The funding is usually disbursed at the beginning of each month, but this may vary depending on the scholarship provider.
6. Can I apply for a scholarship if I am already enrolled in a course?
Yes, it is possible to apply for a scholarship if already enrolled, but the applicant must meet the eligibility criteria and deadlines.
7. What is the role of the scholarship provider in the application process?
The scholarship provider is responsible for reviewing the application, making the selection, and disbursing the funding.
8. Can I appeal the decision if my application is rejected?
Yes, it is possible to appeal the decision, but the applicant must provide a strong justification and new evidence to support the appeal.
