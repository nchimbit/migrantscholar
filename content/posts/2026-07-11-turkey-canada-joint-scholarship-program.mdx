The Turkey and Canada Joint Fully Funded Scholarship Program offers international students a full tuition waiver and a monthly stipend of $1,500 to pursue graduate studies in Turkey and Canada.

## Overview
This scholarship program is designed to foster academic excellence and cultural exchange between Turkey, Canada, and international students. The program provides students with the opportunity to pursue a master's or doctoral degree at a top-ranked university in Turkey or Canada.

## Who Qualifies
To be eligible for the Turkey and Canada Joint Fully Funded Scholarship Program, applicants must fall under one of the following visa categories:
* Study permit
* Work permit
* Refugee claimant
* Protected person
* Temporary resident permit
* Permanent resident
* Express entry

## Available Scholarships
The following scholarships are available under the Turkey and Canada Joint Fully Funded Scholarship Program:
### 1. Turkish Government Scholarship
$1,500 per month, open to international students, deadline varies, https://www.turkiyeburslari.gov.tr/
### 2. Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarship
$50,000 per year, open to international students, deadline November 1, https://vanier.gc.ca/
### 3. Ontario Trillium Scholarship
$40,000 per year, open to international students, deadline varies, https://www.ouac.on.ca/ots/
### 4. University of Toronto Scholarship
$20,000 per year, open to international students, deadline January 15, https://www.utoronto.ca/
### 5. McGill University Scholarship
$15,000 per year, open to international students, deadline January 15, https://www.mcgill.ca/

## Application Steps
To apply for the Turkey and Canada Joint Fully Funded Scholarship Program, follow these steps:
1. Check the eligibility criteria and deadline for the scholarship program.
2. Choose your program of study and university.
3. Prepare your application documents, including transcripts, language proficiency test results, and letters of recommendation.
4. Submit your application online.
5. Pay the application fee, if required.
6. Wait for the application to be processed and reviewed.
7. Receive a conditional acceptance letter and apply for a study permit.
8. Confirm your acceptance and enroll in the program.
