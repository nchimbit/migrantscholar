Are you a migrant or refugee student looking to pursue undergraduate studies in Canada? The University of Toronto is a great option, offering numerous scholarships to support international students. Who qualifies for these scholarships? Generally, international students who demonstrate academic excellence, leadership skills, and a commitment to their community are eligible to apply. Here are some of the University of Toronto scholarships available for international students: The Lester B. Pearson International Scholarship Program, which covers tuition, books, and living expenses, with a deadline of January 15th. The University of Toronto International Scholar Award, which provides $10,000 to $50,000 per year, with a deadline of February 1st. The President's Scholars of Excellence Program, which offers $10,000 in the first year, with a deadline of February 1st. The University of Toronto Mississauga Guaranteed Scholarship, which provides $2,000 to $5,000 per year, with a deadline of February 12th. The University of Toronto Scarborough Guaranteed Scholarship, which offers $1,000 to $5,000 per year, with a deadline of February 12th. To apply for these scholarships, follow these step-by-step instructions: 1. Check your eligibility for each scholarship program. 2. Review the required documents, including transcripts, letters of recommendation, and proof of English proficiency. 3. Submit your application through the University of Toronto's online portal. 4. Upload the required documents and pay the application fee. 5. Monitor your application status and follow up on any additional requirements. Documents checklist: Transcripts from previous academic institutions, Letters of recommendation from teachers or mentors, Proof of English proficiency (e.g., IELTS, TOEFL), Passport copy, Proof of financial support. Frequently Asked Questions: Q: What is the deadline for applying to the University of Toronto scholarships? A: Deadlines vary depending on the scholarship program, but most fall between January 15th and February 12th. Q: Can I apply for multiple scholarships at the same time? A: Yes, you can apply for multiple scholarships, but make sure to review the eligibility criteria and required documents for each program. Q: Do I need to provide proof of English proficiency? A: Yes, proof of English proficiency is required for most scholarship programs, unless you are exempt based on your country of origin or previous academic experience. Q: Can I apply for a scholarship if I am already enrolled at the University of Toronto? A: Some scholarships are only available to incoming students, while others may be open to currently enrolled students. Review the eligibility criteria for each program to determine your eligibility. Q: How will I be notified if I am selected for a scholarship? A: The University of Toronto will notify you through email or mail if you are selected for a scholarship. Make sure to monitor your application status and follow up on any additional requirements. Q: Can I appeal the decision if I am not selected for a scholarship? A: Yes, you can appeal the decision, but you must provide additional information or documentation to support your appeal.
