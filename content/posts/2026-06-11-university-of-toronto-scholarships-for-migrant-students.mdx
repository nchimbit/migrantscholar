## Introduction to University of Toronto Scholarships for Migrant and Refugee Students in Canada
The University of Toronto offers various scholarships to migrant and refugee students in Canada, providing financial assistance to help them pursue their academic goals. These scholarships are designed to support students who have been displaced due to conflict, persecution, or natural disasters, and are now rebuilding their lives in Canada. If you are a migrant or refugee student looking to study at the University of Toronto, you may be eligible for one of these scholarships.

## Who Qualifies
To be eligible for University of Toronto scholarships, migrant and refugee students must hold one of the following visa categories:
* Convention Refugee
* Protected Person
* Refugee Claimant
* Permanent Resident with refugee background
* Temporary Resident Permit holder with refugee background
* Other visa categories may also be eligible, but these are the most common ones.

## Application Steps
To apply for University of Toronto scholarships, follow these steps:
1. Check your eligibility for the scholarship you are interested in.
2. Review the application requirements and deadlines.
3. Submit your application through the University of Toronto's online application portal.
4. Upload all required documents, including transcripts, letters of reference, and proof of language proficiency.
5. Pay the application fee, if required.
6. Submit your application by the deadline.
7. Wait for the scholarship committee to review your application.
8. Receive notification of the outcome of your application.

## Documents Checklist
To apply for University of Toronto scholarships, you will need to provide the following documents:
* Transcripts from all previous academic institutions
* Letters of reference from academic or professional referees
* Proof of language proficiency (e.g. IELTS, TOEFL)
* Proof of financial need (e.g. tax returns, bank statements)
* Proof of refugee status (e.g. refugee claimant document, Convention Refugee document)
* Other documents may be required, depending on the scholarship you are applying for.
