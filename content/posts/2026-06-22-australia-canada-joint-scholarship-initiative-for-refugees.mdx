## Introduction to the Joint Scholarship Initiative
The Australian and Canadian governments have launched a joint scholarship initiative for refugee students pursuing STEM fields, providing an opportunity for them to further their education and build a better future. This initiative is specifically designed for refugee students who are looking to study Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics in Australia or Canada. The initiative aims to support the education and career development of refugee students in these fields, which are in high demand globally.

## Who Qualifies
To be eligible for the joint scholarship initiative, refugee students must hold a valid visa in one of the following categories:
* Permanent Resident Visa (subclass 201)
* Refugee and Humanitarian Visa (subclass 200)
* Temporary Protection Visa (subclass 785)
* Safe Haven Enterprise Visa (subclass 790)
* Protection Visa (subclass 866)
* Permanent Resident Visa (Canada)
* Protected Person Visa (Canada)
* Convention Refugee Visa (Canada)

## Available Scholarships
The joint scholarship initiative offers a range of scholarships for refugee students pursuing STEM fields. Some of the available scholarships include:
### Australian Awards Scholarship
The Australian Awards Scholarship provides coverage of up to $30,000 per year for tuition fees, living expenses, and other study-related costs. To be eligible, applicants must be refugee students from a participating country, have a minimum GPA of 3.5, and meet the English language proficiency requirements. The deadline for applications is 30 April 2026, and further information can be found at https://www.australiaawards.gov.au.
### Canada Graduate Scholarship
The Canada Graduate Scholarship provides coverage of up to $17,500 per year for master's and doctoral students. To be eligible, applicants must be refugee students enrolled in a STEM field at a Canadian university, have a minimum GPA of 3.5, and meet the language proficiency requirements. The deadline for applications is 1 December 2026, and further information can be found at https://www.nserc-crsng.gc.ca.
### International Student Scholarship
The International Student Scholarship provides coverage of up to $10,000 per year for undergraduate and graduate students. To be eligible, applicants must be refugee students enrolled in a STEM field at an Australian or Canadian university, have a minimum GPA of 3.0, and meet the language proficiency requirements. The deadline for applications is 31 March 2026, and further information can be found at https://www.studyinaustralia.gov.au.

The STEM Scholarship Program provides coverage of up to $20,000 per year for undergraduate and graduate students. The University of Melbourne Scholarship provides coverage of up to $25,000 per year for undergraduate and graduate students. To be eligible, applicants must be refugee students enrolled in a STEM field at the University of Melbourne, have a minimum GPA of 3.5, and meet the language proficiency requirements.
