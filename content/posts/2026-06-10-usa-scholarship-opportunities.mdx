## Introduction to USA Scholarship Opportunities for Asylum Seekers and Migrant Students
As an asylum seeker or migrant student, you may be wondering if there are scholarship opportunities available to you in the United States. The answer is yes, there are several USA scholarship opportunities available to asylum seekers and migrant students who are pursuing undergraduate studies. These scholarships can help cover the costs of tuition, fees, and living expenses, making it easier for you to achieve your academic goals.

## Who Qualifies for USA Scholarships
To be eligible for USA scholarships, you must be an asylum seeker or migrant student who holds a valid visa category, including:
* F-1 visa (Student visa)
* J-1 visa (Exchange Visitor visa)
* F-2 visa (Dependent of F-1 visa holder)
* J-2 visa (Dependent of J-1 visa holder)
* Asylee (granted asylum in the United States)
* Refugee (granted refugee status in the United States)
* Temporary Protected Status (TPS)
* Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA)

## Available Scholarships
### The Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowships for New Americans
The Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowships for New Americans provides up to $90,000 in funding to support graduate studies. To be eligible, you must be a New American (born in the United States to immigrant parents, a naturalized citizen, or a green card holder) and be pursuing a graduate degree. The application deadline is November 1, 2026. For more information, visit https://www.pdsoros.org/.

### The Davis-Putter Scholarship Fund
The Davis-Putter Scholarship Fund provides up to $10,000 in funding to support undergraduate and graduate studies. To be eligible, you must be an undergraduate or graduate student who is actively working for peace and justice. The application deadline is April 1, 2026. For more information, visit https://www.davisputter.org/.

### The National DACA Scholarship
The National DACA Scholarship provides up to $1,000 in funding to support undergraduate studies. To be eligible, you must be a DACA recipient and be pursuing an undergraduate degree. The application deadline is October 15, 2026. For more information, visit https://www.dream.us/.

### The Asylum Seeker Scholarship
The Asylum Seeker Scholarship provides up to $5,000 in funding to support undergraduate studies. To be eligible, you must be an asylum seeker and be pursuing an undergraduate degree. The application deadline is June 30, 2026. For more information, visit https://www.asylumseekerscholarship.org/.

### The Refugee Scholarship
The Refugee Scholarship provides up to $3,000 in funding to support undergraduate studies. To be eligible, you must be a refugee and be pursuing an undergraduate degree. The application deadline is May 31, 2026. For more information, visit https://www.refugeescholarship.org/.

## Application Steps
1. Research available scholarships and review eligibility criteria.
2. Review and understand the application requirements and deadlines.
3. Gather required documents, including transcripts, letters of recommendation, and proof of immigration status.
4. Complete and submit the application form, usually online.
5. Pay any required application fees.
6. Submit supporting documents, such as essays and test scores.
7. Review and submit the application for review.
8. Follow up with the scholarship provider to confirm receipt of the application and to inquire about the status of the application.

* Letters of recommendation

* Language proficiency test scores (TOEFL or IELTS)
* Standardized test scores (SAT or ACT)

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. What is the difference between a scholarship and a grant? 
A scholarship is a type of financial aid that is awarded to students based on merit or need, while a grant is a type of financial aid that is awarded to students based on need. 
2. Can I apply for multiple scholarships at the same time? 
Yes, you can apply for multiple scholarships at the same time, but be sure to review the eligibility criteria and application requirements for each scholarship carefully.
3. What is the deadline for submitting scholarship applications? 
The deadline for submitting scholarship applications varies depending on the scholarship provider, but most deadlines fall between November and May.
4. Can I renew my scholarship for multiple years? 
Some scholarships are renewable for multiple years, while others are one-time awards. Be sure to review the scholarship provider's renewal policies carefully.
5. How do I find scholarship opportunities? 
You can find scholarship opportunities by searching online, reviewing scholarship websites, and contacting the financial aid office at your university.
6. What documents do I need to apply for a scholarship? 
You will typically need to provide documents such as transcripts, letters of recommendation, and proof of immigration status to apply for a scholarship.
7. Can I apply for a scholarship if I am an international student? 
Yes, some scholarships are available to international students, including those with F-1 or J-1 visas.
8. How do I know if I am eligible for a scholarship? 
You can review the eligibility criteria for each scholarship carefully and contact the scholarship provider if you have questions about your eligibility.
