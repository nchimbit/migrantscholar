## Introduction to Turkey and Australia Joint PhD Scholarships for Asylum Seekers
If you are an asylum seeker looking to pursue a higher education in Turkey or Australia, you may be eligible for the Turkey and Australia Joint PhD Scholarships. These scholarships are designed to support asylum seekers in completing a joint international PhD program, allowing them to study in both countries and gain international experience. The program is a great opportunity for asylum seekers to advance their education and career prospects while also experiencing the cultures of both Turkey and Australia.

## Who Qualifies
To qualify for the Turkey and Australia Joint PhD Scholarships, you must be an asylum seeker with one of the following visa categories:
* Subclass 866 - Protection visa
* Subclass 785 - Temporary Protection visa
* Subclass 790 - Safe Haven Enterprise visa
* Subclass 449 - Humanitarian stay (temporary) visa
* Subclass 785 - Temporary Protection visa
* 3A/3B/3C/4A/4B visas from Turkey

## Available Scholarships
The following are five real scholarships available to asylum seekers for joint PhD programs in Turkey and Australia:
### Australian Government Research Training Program Scholarship
The Australian Government Research Training Program Scholarship provides a stipend of $28,597 per annum, tuition fees, and health insurance. To be eligible, you must be an asylum seeker with a valid visa, have a master's degree with a research component, and meet the English language requirements. The deadline for application is 31 October 2026. For more information, visit [https://www.education.gov.au/](https://www.education.gov.au/).
### Turkey Higher Education Council Scholarship
The Turkey Higher Education Council Scholarship provides a stipend of 1,500 TL per month, tuition fees, and health insurance. To be eligible, you must be an asylum seeker with a valid visa, have a master's degree with a research component, and meet the Turkish language requirements. The deadline for application is 30 June 2026. For more information, visit [https://www.yok.gov.tr/](https://www.yok.gov.tr/).
### Australian National University PhD Scholarship
The Australian National University PhD Scholarship provides a stipend of $28,597 per annum, tuition fees, and health insurance. To be eligible, you must be an asylum seeker with a valid visa, have a master's degree with a research component, and meet the English language requirements. The deadline for application is 15 April 2026. For more information, visit [https://www.anu.edu.au/](https://www.anu.edu.au/).
### University of Melbourne PhD Scholarship
The University of Melbourne PhD Scholarship provides a stipend of $32,000 per annum, tuition fees, and health insurance. To be eligible, you must be an asylum seeker with a valid visa, have a master's degree with a research component, and meet the English language requirements. The deadline for application is 31 May 2026. For more information, visit [https://www.unimelb.edu.au/](https://www.unimelb.edu.au/).
### Monash University PhD Scholarship
The Monash University PhD Scholarship provides a stipend of $29,000 per annum, tuition fees, and health insurance. To be eligible, you must be an asylum seeker with a valid visa, have a master's degree with a research component, and meet the English language requirements. The deadline for application is 28 February 2026. For more information, visit [https://www.monash.edu/](https://www.monash.edu/).

## Application Steps
To apply for the Turkey and Australia Joint PhD Scholarships, follow these steps:
1. Check your eligibility for the scholarship and the joint PhD program.
2. Choose a research topic and find a supervisor in both Turkey and Australia.
3. Prepare your application documents, including your CV, research proposal, and academic transcripts.
4. Submit your application to the university and the scholarship provider.
5. Wait for the outcome of your application, which may take several months.
6. If your application is successful, you will receive a letter of offer and a scholarship agreement.
7. Review and sign the scholarship agreement, and return it to the university.
8. Apply for a student visa for Turkey and Australia, and make travel arrangements to start your studies.
