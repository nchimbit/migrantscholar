## Introduction to Partially Funded Awards
Partially funded awards are a great way for migrant students to receive financial support for their education, helping to cover tuition fees, living expenses, and other related costs. These awards are typically offered by universities, organizations, and government agencies, and are designed to promote academic excellence and diversity. By applying for partially funded awards, migrant students can reduce their financial burden and focus on achieving their academic goals.

## Who Qualifies
To be eligible for partially funded awards, migrant students must meet certain criteria, including holding a valid visa. The following visa categories are typically eligible:
* F-1 visa (USA)
* J-1 visa (USA)
* F-2 visa (USA)
* J-2 visa (USA)
* Study permit (Canada)
* Temporary Resident Visa (Canada)
* Permanent Resident Visa (Canada)
* Refugee claimant documents (Canada)

The Fulbright Foreign Student Program offers funding of up to $30,000 per year to international students, including migrant students, to pursue a master's or doctoral degree in the USA. To be eligible, applicants must have a strong academic record and demonstrate leadership potential. The deadline for application is October 15th, and the official website can be found at https://foreign.fulbrightonline.org/.

The Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarship provides funding of up to $50,000 per year to outstanding doctoral students, including migrant students, in Canada. The deadline for application is November 1st, and the official website can be found at https://vanier.gc.ca/en/home-accueil.html.

The deadline for application is November 1st, and the official website can be found at https://www.pdsoros.org/.
### 5. Ontario Graduate Scholarship
The Ontario Graduate Scholarship provides funding of up to $15,000 per year to outstanding graduate students, including migrant students, in Ontario, Canada. To be eligible, applicants must have a strong academic record and demonstrate research potential. The deadline for application is February 1st, and the official website can be found at https://osap.gov.on.ca/eng/notol_003157.html.

## Application Steps

1. Research available scholarships and their eligibility criteria.
