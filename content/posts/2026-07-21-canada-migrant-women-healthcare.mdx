The [Canada Graduate Scholarships-Master's program](https://www.nserc-crsng.gc.ca/Students-Etudiants/PG-CS/CGSM-BESCM_eng.asp) exists and migrant women in Canada who are pursuing a master's degree in a healthcare field, such as nursing, qualify for up to $17,500 in funding.

## Overview of Nova Scotia College of Nursing
Migrant women in Canada who are interested in pursuing a career in nursing can benefit from the Nova Scotia College of Nursing. The college offers a range of programs, including diploma and degree programs, that can help migrant women gain the skills and knowledge they need to succeed in the nursing field.

## Who Qualifies
Migrant women who hold a valid [permanent resident visa](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/permanent-resident.html) or a [temporary resident visa](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/visit-canada/temporary-resident-visa.html) with a work permit, study permit, or visitor record, may be eligible for these scholarships.

### Scholarships Available
Here are five real scholarships available to migrant women in Canada pursuing a career in nursing:
1. **[Canada Graduate Scholarships-Master's program](https://www.nserc-crsng.gc.ca/Students-Etudiants/PG-CS/CGSM-BESCM_eng.asp)**: Up to $17,500, master's students, December 1
2. **[Ontario Graduate Scholarship](https://osap.gov.on.ca/eng/notransfer/ogs.htm)**: Up to $15,000, master's and PhD students, varies by institution
3. **[Nova Scotia Student Assistance](https://novascotia.ca/studentassistance/)**: Up to $7,400, undergraduate students, July 31
4. **[Registered Nurses' Foundation of Ontario](https://www.rnfo.org/)**: Up to $2,500, nursing students, April 30
5. **[Canadian Nurses Foundation](https://www.cnf-fiic.ca/)**: Up to $5,000, nursing students, April 30
