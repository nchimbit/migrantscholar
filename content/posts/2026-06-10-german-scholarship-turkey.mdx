## Introduction to DAAD Scholarship for International Students in Turkey
The German Academic Exchange Service (DAAD) offers various scholarships to international students in Turkey, providing them with the opportunity to conduct research and academic exchange in Germany. These scholarships are designed to promote academic cooperation between Turkey and Germany, and to support migrant students in pursuing their academic goals. If you are a migrant student in Turkey looking to further your education or conduct research in Germany, the DAAD scholarship is definitely worth considering.

## Who Qualifies for the DAAD Scholarship
To be eligible for the DAAD scholarship, you must meet certain criteria, including holding a valid visa for Turkey. The following visa categories are eligible for the DAAD scholarship:
* Student visa (visa type D)
* Research visa (visa type D)
* Work visa (visa type D) with a valid work permit
* Family reunion visa (visa type D) with a valid residence permit
* Refugee status or subsidiary protection with a valid residence permit
* Blue Card EU (visa type D) with a valid work permit
* Other visa categories may also be eligible, please check with the DAAD website for more information

## Available DAAD Scholarships
The DAAD offers a range of scholarships for international students in Turkey, each with its own coverage amount, eligibility criteria, and deadline. Here are five real scholarships offered by the DAAD:
### Development-Related Postgraduate Courses (EPOS)
The EPOS scholarship covers a monthly stipend of €750, plus tuition fees and travel costs, and is eligible for students pursuing a master's or PhD degree in a development-related field. The deadline for application is usually in July or August, and more information can be found on the official DAAD website at https://www.daad.de.
### Helmut-Schmidt-Programme (HSP)
The HSP scholarship covers a monthly stipend of €750, plus tuition fees and travel costs, and is eligible for students pursuing a master's degree in a social science or economics field. The deadline for application is usually in July or August, and more information can be found on the official DAAD website at https://www.daad.de.
### Graduate School Scholarship Programme (GSSP)
The GSSP scholarship covers a monthly stipend of €1,200, plus tuition fees and travel costs, and is eligible for students pursuing a PhD degree in a STEM field. The deadline for application is usually in January or February, and more information can be found on the official DAAD website at https://www.daad.de.
### Research Grants (DGSS)
The DGSS scholarship covers a monthly stipend of €1,200, plus travel costs, and is eligible for students pursuing a research project in a STEM field. The deadline for application is usually in January or February, and more information can be found on the official DAAD website at https://www.daad.de.
### Short-Term Research Grant (STRG)
The STRG scholarship covers a monthly stipend of €1,200, plus travel costs, and is eligible for students pursuing a short-term research project in a STEM field. The deadline for application is usually in January or February, and more information can be found on the official DAAD website at https://www.daad.de.

## Application Steps
To apply for a DAAD scholarship, follow these steps:
1. Check your eligibility for the scholarship by reviewing the eligibility criteria on the DAAD website.
2. Choose the scholarship you want to apply for and review the application requirements.
3. Gather all the required documents, including your passport, visa, and academic transcripts.
4. Fill out the online application form on the DAAD website.
5. Upload all the required documents to the online application portal.
6. Submit your application and pay the application fee (if required).
7. Wait for the application to be processed and reviewed by the DAAD.
8. Receive an email notification of the application result, and follow the instructions provided if you are selected for the scholarship.
