As a migrant student looking to pursue higher education in the United States, you may be wondering what international student scholarships are available to you in the USA. The good news is that there are numerous scholarship programs designed specifically for migrant students, offering a range of academic and professional growth opportunities to help you achieve your goals. ## Introduction to International Student Scholarships in the USA
The United States is a popular destination for international students, and migrant students can benefit from a wide range of scholarship programs offered by universities, government agencies, and private organizations. These scholarships can help cover tuition fees, living expenses, and other study-related costs, making it easier for migrant students to pursue their academic and professional goals in the USA.

## Who Qualifies for International Student Scholarships in the USA
To be eligible for international student scholarships in the USA, migrant students typically need to hold a valid visa. The following visa categories are usually eligible for scholarships:
* F-1 visa (Academic Student)
* J-1 visa (Exchange Visitor)
* M-1 visa (Vocational Student)
* H-1B visa (Specialty Occupation)
* L-1 visa (Intracompany Transferee)
* O-1 visa (Individual with Extraordinary Ability)
* TN visa (Trade NAFTA)
* TD visa (Trade NAFTA Dependent)

## Available Scholarships for Migrant Students in the USA
Here are five scholarships that migrant students can apply for:
### Fulbright Scholarship
The Fulbright Scholarship is a prestigious award that covers full tuition fees, living expenses, and travel costs. To be eligible, applicants must have a bachelor's degree, proficiency in English, and a strong academic record. The deadline for applications is October 15, and the official website is [https://fulbright.org/](https://fulbright.org/).
### MasterCard Foundation Scholarship
The MasterCard Foundation Scholarship provides full tuition fees, living expenses, and a stipend for migrant students pursuing a master's degree. Eligible applicants must have a bachelor's degree, a strong academic record, and demonstrate leadership skills. The deadline for applications is February 1, and the official website is [https://mastercardfdn.org/](https://mastercardfdn.org/).
### Humphrey Fellowship Program
The Humphrey Fellowship Program offers a full scholarship covering tuition fees, living expenses, and travel costs for migrant students pursuing a master's degree in a field related to public service. Eligible applicants must have a bachelor's degree, a strong academic record, and at least five years of work experience. The deadline for applications is October 1, and the official website is [https://www.humphreyfellowship.org/](https://www.humphreyfellowship.org/).
### Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowship
The Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowship provides a scholarship of up to $90,000 for migrant students pursuing a graduate degree in the USA. Eligible applicants must have a bachelor's degree, a strong academic record, and demonstrate leadership skills. The deadline for applications is November 1, and the official website is [https://pdsoros.org/](https://pdsoros.org/).
### Rotary Peace Fellowship
The Rotary Peace Fellowship offers a full scholarship covering tuition fees, living expenses, and travel costs for migrant students pursuing a master's degree in a field related to peace and conflict resolution. Eligible applicants must have a bachelor's degree, a strong academic record, and at least three years of work experience. The deadline for applications is May 31, and the official website is [https://www.rotary.org/en/peace-fellowships](https://www.rotary.org/en/peace-fellowships).

## Application Steps for International Student Scholarships in the USA
To apply for international student scholarships in the USA, follow these steps:
1. Research and identify the scholarships that you are eligible for.
2. Review the eligibility criteria and application requirements for each scholarship.
3. Prepare your application documents, including transcripts, letters of recommendation, and a personal statement.
4. Submit your application online or by mail, depending on the scholarship requirements.
5. Take the required English language proficiency tests, such as TOEFL or IELTS.
6. Apply for a student visa, if required.
7. Receive and review your scholarship award letter, if selected.
8. Accept the scholarship award and prepare to begin your studies in the USA.

* Visa application documents (if required)
