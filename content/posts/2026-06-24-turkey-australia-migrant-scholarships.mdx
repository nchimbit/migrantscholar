## Introduction to Government Scholarships for Migrant Students
As a migrant student, you may be wondering what government support is available to help you pursue higher education in Turkey or Australia. The Turkish and Australian governments offer various scholarships to migrant students, providing financial assistance to help cover tuition fees, living expenses, and other study-related costs. These scholarships are designed to support the educational and career goals of migrant students, promoting cultural diversity and academic excellence in both countries.

## Who Qualifies for Government Scholarships
To be eligible for government scholarships in Turkey or Australia, migrant students must hold a valid visa that falls under one of the following categories:
* Subclass 500 (Student) visa
* Subclass 457 (Temporary Work (Skilled)) visa
* Subclass 482 (Temporary Skilled Work) visa
* Subclass 485 (Temporary Graduate) visa
* Temporary Protection visa (TPV)
* Safe Haven Enterprise visa (SHEV)
* Humanitarian visa

## Available Scholarships for Migrant Students
The following scholarships are available to migrant students in Turkey and Australia:
### Turkey Scholarships
The Turkish government offers various scholarships to international students, including migrant students. Some of the notable scholarships include:
* Name: Türkiye Scholarships
* Coverage amount: Full tuition fee waiver, monthly stipend, and accommodation
* Eligibility: Migrant students who have been accepted into a Turkish university
* Deadline: February 20, 2026
* Official website: https://www.turkiyeburslari.gov.tr/
### Australia Scholarships
The Australian government also offers several scholarships to migrant students, including:
* Name: Australia Awards Scholarships
* Coverage amount: Full tuition fee waiver, living expenses, and return airfare
* Eligibility: Migrant students who have been accepted into an Australian university
* Deadline: April 30, 2026
* Official website: https://www.australiaawards.gov.au/
* Name: Research Training Program (RTP) Scholarships
* Coverage amount: Tuition fee waiver and living stipend
* Eligibility: Migrant students who have been accepted into a research program at an Australian university
* Deadline: October 31, 2026
* Official website: https://www.education.gov.au/research-training-program
* Name: Australian Government Research Training Program (AGRTP) Scholarships
* Coverage amount: Tuition fee waiver and living stipend
* Eligibility: Migrant students who have been accepted into a research program at an Australian university
* Deadline: August 31, 2026
* Official website: https://www.uq.edu.au/grad-school/scholarships
* Name: International Postgraduate Research Scholarship (IPRS)
* Coverage amount: Tuition fee waiver and living stipend
* Eligibility: Migrant students who have been accepted into a research program at an Australian university
* Deadline: October 31, 2026
* Official website: https://www.education.gov.au/international-postgraduate-research-scholarship

## Application Steps for Government Scholarships
To apply for government scholarships in Turkey or Australia, migrant students should follow these steps:
1. Check the eligibility criteria for the scholarship
2. Gather all required documents, including academic transcripts and language proficiency test results
3. Submit an application to the university or institution
4. Apply for a visa, if required
5. Complete and submit the scholarship application form
6. Provide additional documentation, such as a personal statement and letters of recommendation
7. Wait for the application to be processed and a decision to be made
8. Accept the scholarship offer, if successful

## Documents Checklist for Scholarship Applications
Migrant students should ensure they have the following documents ready when applying for government scholarships:
* Academic transcripts
* Language proficiency test results (e.g. IELTS or TOEFL)
* Passport
* Visa (if applicable)
* Personal statement
* Letters of recommendation
* Proof of acceptance into a university or institution
* Other relevant documents, such as a CV or research proposal
