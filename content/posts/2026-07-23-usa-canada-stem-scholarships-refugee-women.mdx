The [Margaret McNamara Memorial Fund](https://www.mmfund.org/) scholarship exists for refugee women, providing up to $15,000 in funding for STEM education in the USA and Canada.

## Who Qualifies
Refugee women who hold a valid visa, such as F-1, J-1, or asylum status, are eligible to apply for these scholarships. Applicants must also demonstrate a strong interest in pursuing a career in STEM fields. Visa categories that qualify include F-1, J-1, F-2, J-2, asylum status, and refugee status. For more information on [scholarships for refugees](https://migrantscholar.vercel.app/by-eligibility/refugees), please visit our website.

## Available Scholarships
### Margaret McNamara Memorial Fund
* Amount: up to $15,000
* Eligibility: refugee women pursuing STEM education
* Deadline: January 15, 2027
* Official URL: https://www.mmfund.org/
### Society of Women Engineers Scholarship
* Amount: up to $10,000
* Eligibility: women pursuing STEM education, including refugees
* Deadline: February 28, 2027

### Google Anita Borg Memorial Scholarship
* Amount: up to $10,000
* Eligibility: women pursuing STEM education, including refugees
* Deadline: February 1, 2027
* Official URL: https://www.google.com/education/scholarships/
### Microsoft Scholarship
* Amount: up to $20,000
* Eligibility: students pursuing STEM education, including refugees
* Deadline: March 31, 2027
* Official URL: https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/diversity/programs/microsoft-scholarships
### [NSERC Canada](https://www.nserc-crsng.gc.ca/) Scholarship
* Amount: up to $17,500
* Eligibility: students pursuing STEM education in Canada, including refugees
* Deadline: October 15, 2026
* Official URL: https://www.nserc-crsng.gc.ca/

3. Fill out the online application form for each scholarship
4. Submit the application form and supporting documents
5. Wait for the review process to be completed
6. Receive notification of the scholarship decision
7. Accept the scholarship award, if offered
8. Use the scholarship funding to pursue STEM education

## Documents Checklist
* Transcripts from previous academic institutions
* Letters of recommendation from professors or supervisors
* Personal statement outlining career goals and reasons for pursuing STEM education
* Proof of refugee status or valid visa
* Proof of admission to a STEM program at a university in the USA or Canada
