## Introduction to Fully Funded Undergraduate Awards for Asylum Seekers in the USA
As an asylum seeker in the United States, you may be wondering if there are fully funded undergraduate awards available to help you pursue your higher education goals. The answer is yes, there are several scholarships and awards available to asylum seekers in the USA that can cover the full cost of an undergraduate degree. These awards are designed to support students who have been displaced or are seeking refuge in the United States and are eager to pursue their education.

## Who Qualifies
To qualify for these fully funded undergraduate awards, asylum seekers must meet certain eligibility criteria, including holding a specific visa category. The following visa categories are typically eligible:
* Refugee (includes individuals admitted to the United States as refugees under Section 207 of the Immigration and Nationality Act)
* Asylee (includes individuals granted asylum under Section 208 of the Immigration and Nationality Act)
* Special Immigrant Visa (SIV) holder
* Temporary Protected Status (TPS) holder
* Deferred Enforced Departure (DED) holder
* Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA) recipient

## Available Scholarships
There are several fully funded undergraduate awards available to asylum seekers in the USA, including:
### The Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowships for New Americans
The Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowships for New Americans provides up to $90,000 in funding to cover the cost of an undergraduate degree. To be eligible, applicants must be 30 years old or younger and have been born outside of the United States or have been naturalized as a US citizen. The deadline to apply is November 1st of each year. For more information, visit [https://www.pdsoros.org](https://www.pdsoros.org).
### The HIAS Scholarship
The HIAS Scholarship provides up to $10,000 in funding to cover the cost of an undergraduate degree. To be eligible, applicants must be refugees or asylum seekers who have been resettled in the United States. The deadline to apply is March 15th of each year. For more information, visit [https://www.hias.org](https://www.hias.org).
### The Refugee Scholarship
The Refugee Scholarship provides up to $5,000 in funding to cover the cost of an undergraduate degree. To be eligible, applicants must be refugees or asylum seekers who are currently enrolled in an undergraduate degree program in the United States. The deadline to apply is June 1st of each year. For more information, visit [https://www.refugeescholarship.org](https://www.refugeescholarship.org).
### The Asylum Seeker Scholarship
The Asylum Seeker Scholarship provides up to $8,000 in funding to cover the cost of an undergraduate degree. To be eligible, applicants must be asylum seekers who are currently enrolled in an undergraduate degree program in the United States. The deadline to apply is September 1st of each year. For more information, visit [https://www.asylumseekerscholarship.org](https://www.asylumseekerscholarship.org).
### The New American Scholarship
The New American Scholarship provides up to $12,000 in funding to cover the cost of an undergraduate degree. To be eligible, applicants must be refugees or asylum seekers who have been resettled in the United States and are currently enrolled in an undergraduate degree program. The deadline to apply is January 15th of each year. For more information, visit [https://www.newamericanscholarship.org](https://www.newamericanscholarship.org).

## Application Steps
To apply for these fully funded undergraduate awards, asylum seekers should follow these steps:
1. Review the eligibility criteria for each scholarship to determine which ones you are eligible for.
2. Gather all required documents, including your visa, passport, and transcripts.
3. Submit your application by the deadline, making sure to include all required documents and information.
4. Follow up with the scholarship organization to confirm that your application has been received.
5. Prepare for any interviews or assessments that may be required as part of the application process.
6. Wait to hear back from the scholarship organization regarding the status of your application.
7. If selected, review and accept the scholarship offer, making sure to understand the terms and conditions.
8. Use the scholarship funding to cover the cost of your undergraduate degree.
